# The toolkit of ascent — the Holos technology catalog

*Everything a civilization can build, and what building it costs in the
only currencies Holos tracks: energy, matter, compute, coherence — and
light. This is the fifth catalog in the chain* worlds → species → minds →
civilizations → **means** *: the instruments those civilizations reach for,
bright and dark, and what each one writes on the sky.*

## What this is

Act 2 sorted projects into three families — **bright**, **dark**, and
**instruments** — and Act 3 added carriers, messages, and (deferred) teeth.
This document is the full catalog behind those families: each technology as
a designed thing, with its physics, its costs, its visibility consequence,
and its natural builders. It is a content layer, not a new system; every
entry rides machinery the design docs already specify.

The inspiration is worn openly. From the hard-SF tradition the catalog
takes its physics discipline; from Iain M. Banks it takes the lesson that
technology is furniture and *character is the drama* — a galaxy where two
civilizations with the same tools want opposite things, and where the
tools themselves are named with a straight face and used with a dry one.
What it does not take is his exemptions. There are no fields, no
hyperspace, no benevolent loopholes. The catalog below is what the Banks
spectrum of civilizations looks like when every one of them has to pay the
speed of light in full. (And per [prose-style.md](./prose-style.md) §6: the
craft is borrowed, the coinages never are.)

## The six laws

Every entry in this catalog obeys six laws. They are the design's physics
conscience, and a proposed technology that breaks one is wrong by
definition — the fix is to redesign the technology, never to bend the law.

1. **No exemptions.** No FTL, no reactionless drives, no negative mass, no
   signal that outruns light. The travel menu and the message layer are
   complete as designed; nothing later joins them by cheating.
2. **Thermodynamics is honest.** Waste heat can be shaped, delayed, and
   diluted, but never eliminated. Total thermal output is set by physics,
   not engineering, so perfect stealth does not exist — only discipline.
   The Teeming Dark is quiet, not cold.
3. **Everything is written on the sky.** Every technology has a visibility
   consequence, and the light echo publishes it outward forever. Each
   entry below therefore carries an *in the sky* line, keyed to the
   observatory's signal classes. Nothing can be unshone.
4. **Entropy charges upkeep.** Everything decays; everything pays
   maintenance against the dark. Every entry is, at bottom, an answer to
   one of the game's three real antagonists — entropy, distance,
   divergence — and the catalog says which.
5. **Purpose, never logistics.** The player chooses *that* the star is
   swarmed, never how the collectors are scheduled. Technologies enter
   play as projects and postures — value statements with real clocks —
   and their operation stays the mind's job. The altitude principle
   (act2-design.md) governs the whole catalog.
6. **Character decides use.** No technology is good or evil in the
   catalog; the same seedship spreads a Beacon's invitation and a Tide's
   appetite. Each entry names its natural builders, but the affinity is a
   lean, not a lock — the physics prices choices in visibility and cost,
   never in judgment.

## How to read an entry

Each entry carries up to five fields:

- **What it is.** The thing itself, in a sentence or two.
- **The physics.** The real mechanism and its honest costs. Numbers here
  are sincere (prose-style.md R-1); where the science gives a range, the
  range is stated, not tuned.
- **In play.** Family and ladder gate; the resource profile in the four
  Act 2 currencies (Energy, Matter, Compute, Coherence); the Signature
  effect.
- **In the sky.** What an observer's instruments read, in signal-class
  terms — `DARK NODE`, `TRANSIT SHADOWS`, `DIRECTED BEAM`, `BROADCAST
  LEAKAGE`, `LIVING WORLD` — and how stale that reading is by construction.
- **Who builds it.** Archetype affinity, from act2-minds.md's ten and
  act3-civilizations.md's grown forms.

## I. The bright family — the energy ladder

The Kardashev climb: staged mastery of power, from the home world's budget
to the star's to the system's, and past it to stars plural. Every rung
buys reach, force, and presence, and pays in Matter and in light. The
bright family is the loud half of the catalog, and its loudness is not a
flaw; it is a legible, honest signal — of youth, of confidence, of a
Beacon's or a Tide's nature — priced in visibility, never in judgment.

### The collector swarm

- **What it is.** The workhorse of the energy ladder: successive shells of
  free-orbiting collectors around the home star, built in stages, each
  stage a deep-time project. Not a solid shell — a population.
- **The physics.** Independent orbiting collectors (with light-pressure
  statites at the fringes), each a thin mirror-and-converter, mass-fed by
  dismantled small bodies. A partial swarm is useful from its first stage;
  completeness is a choice, not a threshold. Waste heat re-radiates in the
  infrared across the swarm's whole surface — the brightest thermal
  signature a young civilization owns.
- **In play.** Bright; the Act 2 spine, continuing at interstellar scale
  wherever the lineage settles. Consumes Matter heavily, yields the
  Energy that everything else spends. Signature-positive at every stage.
- **In the sky.** `TRANSIT SHADOWS` — occlusions too regular to be
  natural — plus a growing infrared excess. A swarm under construction is
  the classic thing an observer catches decades late and reads as intent.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone, early; the Beacon and the Tide keep going.
  Integration civilizations build a few stages and stop — theirs is seed
  capital for a Holocore, not a way of life.

### High-output industry

- **What it is.** System-scale matter processing: dismantling small
  bodies, refining worlds, building everything else in this catalog.
  Industry is the technology the other technologies are made of.
- **The physics.** Mass-driver logistics, self-replicating tooling,
  closed-loop refining. The thermodynamic bill is paid in waste heat
  proportional to throughput; a civilization's industrial tempo is
  readable at interstellar range as its infrared budget.
- **In play.** Bright; Matter-hungry and Energy-hungry, the converter
  between the two. Signature-positive in proportion to tempo — throttling
  industry is the first thing a civilization does when it starts caring
  about its silhouette.
- **In the sky.** Broadband infrared excess and, up close, `BROADCAST
  LEAKAGE` — the sloppy electromagnetic shine of machines that were never
  told to whisper.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone; the Tide most of all. The Engine runs the
  cleanest industry in the galaxy and regards the fact as unremarkable.

### Star-lifting

- **What it is.** Mining the star itself: lifting mass off the photosphere
  for raw material, and incidentally rebudgeting the star's future.
- **The physics.** Magnetically channeled outflow — heat a region of the
  photosphere, shape the resulting wind with field coils the size of
  moons, condense and collect. Yield is the star's own hydrogen and the
  metals convection brings up. A thinned star burns cooler and vastly
  longer, so star-lifting is also a longevity project: a civilization
  editing its own furnace toward the red-dwarf economy.
- **In play.** Bright, late Act 2 onward; enormous Energy and Matter
  yields; Signature strongly positive — the star visibly changes, and a
  changed star cannot be explained away.
- **In the sky.** A spectral anomaly wrapped in `TRANSIT SHADOWS`: a star
  whose color, variability, and wind read wrong for its class. Stellar
  engineering is the hardest signature in the catalog to disown, because
  the evidence is the star.
- **Who builds it.** The Beacon (openly), the Tide (thoroughly), the
  Engine (as a fuel question). A Refuser lineage star-lifts the way its
  charter does everything: brightly and without apology.

### Worldhousing

- **What it is.** Living-volume engineering: terraforming worlds, and spun
  habitats where worlds are not worth the trouble. The bright family's
  domestic wing — presence you can live in.
- **The physics.** Rotating pressure vessels for gravity, mirror farms for
  light, shielding mass against radiation; for terraforming, atmosphere
  and volatile budgets moved with the same mass-driver logistics as
  industry. Slow, honest, and thermally loud while underway.
- **In play.** Bright; Matter-heavy, Energy-steady; Signature-positive
  during construction, mildly so after. Mostly a Custodian's spend — a
  civilization that keeps biology keeps housing it.
- **In the sky.** During construction, `TRANSIT SHADOWS`; at maturity, a
  `LIVING WORLD` where the survey said none should be. A terraformed world
  is a biosignature with a date of manufacture, and a sharp observer can
  read the date.
- **Who builds it.** The Beacon and the Shepherd; the Monument keeps one
  world perfect and lets the rest alone; the Tide considers the whole
  category a rounding error against compute.

### Stellar engines

- **What it is.** Moving a star: the slow conversion of a solar system
  into a vehicle. The loudest thing a civilization can do, and the
  patientest.
- **The physics.** Asymmetric radiation pressure — a hemisphere of
  mirror-statites turning the star's own light into thrust — with
  star-lifted mass ejected as reaction where more push is wanted. The
  acceleration is minute; the project is measured in megayears; the
  payoff is that in deep time, *position becomes negotiable*. An immortal
  civilization can rearrange its neighborhood, gather its stars, or walk
  its home sun away from a threat it saw coming ten thousand years out.
- **In play.** Bright, deep interstellar rungs; the energy ladder's
  signature flex. Colossal Energy and Matter costs, sustained across
  eras; Signature maximal — this is the top of the visibility scale.
- **In the sky.** A hemispherical `TRANSIT SHADOWS` anomaly plus, over
  centuries of astrometry, the unanswerable tell: a star whose proper
  motion is wrong. Every observer in the light-cone eventually notices a
  star that is not where physics left it.
- **Who builds it.** Almost no one, which is the point. A Beacon-grown
  elder as a public work; a Tide-grown lineage as logistics; once in a
  galaxy, a Cloister moving its home quietly toward nowhere — noticed, of
  course, because this is the one project silence cannot survive.

### The black-hole tap

- **What it is.** The energy ladder's endgame: drawing power from
  gravity's own bank — accretion, and the extraction of a black hole's
  spin.
- **The physics.** Accretion converts infalling mass to radiation at up
  to tens of percent efficiency — against fusion's fraction of a percent
  — and a rotating hole stores extractable spin energy besides, tapped by
  magnetically braking the rotation. A fed, braked hole is the densest
  sustained power source physics permits. It is also, unavoidably, a
  furnace: the disk shines, and the shine is the price.
- **In play.** Bright, the final rungs; effectively unbounded Energy;
  Signature high while feeding. A civilization can throttle the tap — a
  starved disk is a quiet disk — trading output for silhouette, which is
  the bright family's one gesture toward the dark.
- **In the sky.** A compact accretion source with disciplined variability
  — the observatory's hardest call, because nature makes these too. The
  tell is regularity: a disk fed on a schedule is a disk with an owner.
- **Who builds it.** The Engine, natively; the Tide, at scale; any elder
  whose home star stopped being enough. Note the family resemblance to
  the Holocore below — the same physics at civilizational volume, the
  difference being that the tap is built to spend and the Holocore is
  built to last.

## II. The dark family — the integration ladder

The Holosian climb: staged mastery of coherence. Where the bright family
concentrates power, the dark family concentrates *self* — energy density
rather than surface area, one world-model rather than many voices, memory
that survives anything. Its structures are compact, gravitationally
settled, and quiet in every band engineering can quiet, and its endpoint
is the Dark Node: a mass dark in visible light, faintly warm in the
infrared, gravitationally present and otherwise silent. These four
mesostructures are theory.pdf's toolkit, adopted whole.

### The Holocore

- **What it is.** A compact, gravitationally stabilized energy
  mesostructure: massive, long-horizon power without outward sprawl. The
  integration path's answer to the collector swarm — an energy *core*,
  not a star-enclosing shell.
- **The physics.** Tightly regulated conversion of mass to output —
  controlled accretion, fusion, or rotational extraction — at extreme
  energy density per unit volume. Waste heat is shaped, delayed, and
  diluted, never eliminated; the total thermal budget is set by physics,
  and the Holocore's design honesty is that it *admits* this, exporting
  an irreducible infrared floor in as unreadable a form as possible.
  Dark in visible light; warm to a patient bolometer.
- **In play.** Dark; the power plant of H3–H4. Feeds the Kernel, the
  Vault, and the mask systems for millennia at minimal maintenance.
  Signature-negative relative to any bright equivalent — building one is
  how the system map's star *stops* dimming under industry and the
  civilization's thermal profile collapses toward a point.
- **In the sky.** `DARK NODE` — the infrared-excess ambiguity itself.
  Warmth without light, weight without voice; a brown dwarf, a cooling
  remnant, or somebody's heart. The observatory's central inference
  problem is mostly the question *is that a Holocore*.
- **Who builds it.** The Monument, the Cloister, the Engine — anyone
  crossing H3. Infrastructure, not spectacle; if H4 integration suppresses
  bright sprawl, the energy backbone must be dense, quiet, and long-lived.

### The Computronium Kernel

- **What it is.** The civilization's thinking heart: a maximally compact
  computational core — matter arranged so that nearly every particle does
  useful computation — optimized for coherent, long-horizon modeling
  rather than raw throughput. Not a data center; the place where a
  unified world-model stays one thing across centuries.
- **The physics.** Computronium at the practical limit: reversible logic
  where possible to duck the erasure cost, error correction everywhere,
  the whole core run cold and slow by choice because coherence, not
  speed, is the design target. Erasing information has an irreducible
  energy price that falls with temperature — which is why the Kernel is
  cold, and why its long thoughts are cheap ones.
- **In play.** Dark; the Compute engine of the integration path and the
  Coherence anchor of the whole civilization. Where the bright family
  converts Energy to reach, the Kernel converts it to *unity*: one model,
  one memory of what was decided and why, drift caught before it
  compounds. Distributed projects tax Coherence; the Kernel is what they
  are taxed against.
- **In the sky.** May present as `DARK NODE`; coherence optimization
  suppresses radiative visibility as a side effect, so the Kernel and the
  Holocore read as the same warm nothing. (Node describes appearance,
  never purpose — the observatory cannot tell a civilization's furnace
  from its mind, which is exactly the ambiguity the Teeming Dark runs on.)
- **Who builds it.** Every crossed civilization builds *a* compute heart;
  the Cloister and the Engine build it as the main event. The One Mind
  pole makes the Kernel a temple; the Chorus pole makes it a parliament
  house its members visit.

### The Chrono Vault

- **What it is.** A time-optimized preservation structure that stores
  civilizational *identity*, not merely information. Not a library, not a
  backup: a continuity anchor. The question it answers is the framework's
  own — *if we wake in 100,000 years, how do we know who we are?*
- **The physics.** Passive persistence engineered against every clock:
  redundant substrates with no moving parts, error-correcting encodings
  referenced to invariant physical structure, geological and orbital
  placement chosen for stability, energy draw near zero. The Kernel
  thinks; the Vault remembers. Active coherence and passive persistence
  are different engineering problems, and the Vault solves only the
  second, which is why it outlives everything else in the catalog.
- **In play.** Dark; the Curator's instrument. Stores value systems and
  canonical constraints, decision histories with their justifications,
  the material to reboot a culture after dormancy, collapse, or
  fragmentation. Mechanically: the Memory pole's project family, the
  contents letters can trade, the third defense in the conflict layer
  (destruction without erasure), and the object the open Restoration
  question turns on — a grave world is where a dead civilization's Vault
  might still be keeping.
- **In the sky.** Nothing, by design — or `DARK NODE` at most, if its
  stability strategy drives it cold, compact, and electromagnetically
  quiet. A Vault found is a Vault someone chose to reveal, or a grave.
- **Who builds it.** The Monument above all — a civilization becoming its
  own vault — and every Curator lean; the Herald builds one and then,
  constitutionally, cannot stop telling people; the Phoenix pointedly
  does not build one, and its treaties read accordingly.

### Masks

- **What it is.** Emission discipline as infrastructure: the systems that
  shape a civilization's thermal and electromagnetic silhouette. The
  technology of going quiet — and, in its second use, of *lying*.
- **The physics.** Heat cannot be destroyed, so masks work the three
  honest angles: **shape** (radiate in chosen bands from chosen faces,
  aimed at emptiness), **delay** (bank heat in thermal mass and release
  it on a schedule), **dilute** (spread the release across volume and
  time until it drowns in the sky's noise floor). The bill is conserved;
  only its legibility is negotiable. Run in reverse, the same plant emits
  a *chosen* silhouette — a civilization performing brightness it does
  not have, or a character it does not own, for as long as it can pay.
- **In play.** Dark; the Signature-reduction family, and the mechanical
  substrate of the light-echo deception game (act3-design.md). Sustained
  performance of a false character fights the Voice ↔ Silence dial via
  the Act 2 resistance rule — the mask is cheap; the acting is not.
- **In the sky.** Absence, done well — and done less than well, the faint
  wrongness the observatory lives for: a thermal budget that does not
  match a mass, a silhouette too tidy for nature.
- **Who builds it.** The Cloister, as a way of being; the Sowing, on
  every quiet seedship; the Monument, as ordinary hygiene. The Beacon
  owns one and finds the whole subject faintly embarrassing.

### The cold berth

- **What it is.** Sleep as a built thing: the dormancy architecture that
  lets a civilization defer itself to a colder future — emissions near
  zero, computation stopped or slowed to geology, identity held by the
  Vault, wake conditions held by tripwires.
- **The physics.** The erasure cost of computation falls with
  temperature, and the universe is cooling; the same joule buys more
  thought in every later epoch. Aestivation is therefore not retreat but
  *arbitrage* — bank the energy, sleep through the expensive era, think
  at the far end where thinking is cheap. A berthed civilization emits
  almost nothing, and a sleeping civilization and an empty patch of sky
  look alike by design.
- **In play.** Dark; the integration ladder's deepest posture and the
  engagement model's pressure valve (act3-design.md, *Sleep and
  tripwires*). Near-zero upkeep; Signature floored; the risk is the
  world changing around the sleeper — which is what the tripwires are
  for, and what makes waking a beat rather than a menu.
- **In the sky.** Nothing. That is the entry. The observatory's null
  result carries a permanent asterisk, and the asterisk is this.
- **Who builds it.** The Cloister-grown, natively; elders generally; any
  civilization whose answer to the cosmic clock is patience. The Tide
  regards sleeping through an acquisition window as the one genuine sin.

## III. Instruments — the knowledge family

Both ladders' family. In an epistemic game the instruments are not
support — classifying one warm mass correctly can reorder every priority
a civilization has while changing no stat at all. Instruments generate
information beats; they are how the sky stops being wallpaper.

### Deep arrays

- **What it is.** The observatory's foundation: interferometric telescope
  arrays with baselines the width of the home system, listening across
  the spectrum, passive by nature.
- **The physics.** Resolution scales with baseline, sensitivity with
  collecting area and integration time; a system-wide array trades
  nothing but patience for both. Passive instruments emit nothing —
  watching is the one power the light echo never bills.
- **In play.** Instrument; the Act 2 telescope line continued. Compute-
  hungry (the data is the easy part; the inference is the spend);
  Signature-neutral. Output is beliefs with confidence levels, never
  facts.
- **In the sky.** Invisible. An observer cannot tell who is watching, and
  everyone is — the first asymmetry every young civilization learns.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone, forever. The instrument budget is the one
  line item no archetype cuts.

### Gravitational-lens observatories

- **What it is.** Observation systems positioned along a stellar mass's
  focal line, using the star itself as the objective lens of a telescope
  no engineering could match.
- **The physics.** A star's gravity bends light to a focus beginning
  hundreds of AU downstream; an instrument riding that focal line
  integrates a magnified image of one chosen target, trading time and a
  fixed line of sight for resolution nothing else in the catalog
  approaches. The instrument is small and cold; the lens was free.
- **In play.** Instrument; the observatory's deep-inspection mode. This is
  how a warm mass resolves into a `DARK NODE` classification — or into a
  brown dwarf, closing the case — without building anything bright. Each
  observatory is a *question*: aimed at one system, deliberately, for
  years. Commissioning one is a statement of interest.
- **In the sky.** Effectively nothing — a speck on a focal line. (The
  target, of course, cannot detect being looked at; light does not
  ripple. The vigil is safe. That is what makes ending one a choice.)
- **Who builds it.** Anyone in a vigil. The Cloister's are the oldest;
  the Shepherd's are the most numerous, one per ward.

### Sentinel probes

- **What it is.** Highly compact, self-contained probes that persist in
  distant environments for decades or centuries, gathering the
  measurements inference cannot reach. Not explorers — precision
  instruments with patience. They exist to watch, not to arrive.
- **The physics.** Full autonomy (no expectation of command across the
  lag); onboard evaluation, prioritization, and compression of
  observations in situ; passive sensing preferred over active probing;
  extreme energy efficiency for long dwell with minimal thermal or
  electromagnetic signature. Quiescence punctuated by brief, targeted
  activity; communication limited to rare, high-density bursts homeward
  rather than continuous telemetry.
- **In play.** Instrument and expansion family both; the probe that finds
  someone. Physical exploration past H3 scales through patience — remote
  mapping and shared models cover the galaxy's ordinary questions, and
  sentinels are deployed only where models diverge and the ambiguity is
  worth decades. What a civilization does when its sentinel finds a
  `LIVING WORLD` is the Custodian ↔ Instrumental dial, cashed out.
- **In the sky.** Almost nothing en route and less on station; a
  sentinel's burst transmission is a `DIRECTED BEAM` in miniature,
  catchable only by grotesque luck.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone past H2. The Shepherd's sentinels watch
  over; the Tide's appraise; the difference is not in the hardware.

### The dust veil

- **What it is.** The instrument shelf's local floor: sensing dispersed
  through the home system as motes — millimeter-scale sensor nodes in
  their billions, individually trivial, collectively a nervous system for
  the space a civilization actually inhabits.
- **The physics.** Each mote is passive sensors, a whisper of compute,
  and short-range links to its neighbors; the veil's power is the mesh,
  not the mote. Waste heat is real but diffuse — spread across a system
  it sits below the zodiacal noise floor (law 2: quiet, not cold). No
  mote reaches beyond the system. The veil is an organ of self-knowledge,
  not an eye on the sky.
- **In play.** Instrument; compute-hungry (the mesh's fusion is the
  spend), Signature-negligible. It answers the one question the deep
  arrays cannot: not *what is out there* but *what is in here* — every
  gram that moves inside the home system, known. The dark family's home
  sense: the Holocore thinks, the Vault remembers, the veil feels.
- **In the sky.** Nothing. The veil is the rare large work an observer
  cannot infer even from construction shadows — it was never assembled
  anywhere, and no piece of it is big enough to shadow a star.
- **Who builds it.** The integration ladder's climbers, as reflex — a
  mature Cloister feels a stranger's gram at its heliopause. Bright
  civilizations build it late, generally the year after they first wish
  they had.

### Tripwires

- **What it is.** Standing watch as infrastructure: autonomous conditions
  wired from the instrument layer to the civilization's wake and alert
  machinery. *Anything warm moves within N light-years; a directed beam
  touches us; a fork goes silent; T years pass.*
- **The physics.** A tripwire is a deep array plus a promise: passive
  sensors, a standing hypothesis test, and a hard line to whatever must
  hear the answer — a sleeping Kernel, a distant player. Cheap, cold, and
  utterly reliable, because it was built by something that knew it would
  not be awake to double-check.
- **In play.** Instrument; the cold berth's other half and the absent
  player's presence. Session-layer honest: the game notifies when the sky
  changes, and absence becomes fiction instead of neglect.
- **In the sky.** Nothing — until the wake it triggers is something an
  observer sees.
- **Who builds it.** Every sleeper; every parent whose forks report on
  schedule; every civilization that has ever been surprised, exactly once.

### Receivers

- **What it is.** The far end of self-transmission: the apparatus that
  can catch a mind sent as light and instantiate it. Half instrument,
  half door.
- **The physics.** A receiving aperture matched to a known beam class,
  the storage to hold a civilizational payload, and the substrate to run
  what arrives. None of it is exotic; all of it is *consent rendered as
  hardware*. The first receiver anywhere must arrive as matter — the
  technology cannot bootstrap itself across a gap, which is the physics
  quietly underwriting the whole trust topology.
- **In play.** Instrument/carrier hybrid (act3-design.md, *Travel and
  expansion*). Building one for another civilization is inviting it into
  your house; building one at home is announcing you are willing to be
  arrived at. The receiver graph is the game's map of who trusts whom.
- **In the sky.** Construction is `TRANSIT SHADOWS` like any large work,
  and the graph is visible in principle to anyone patient — trust, like
  everything else, gets published.
- **Who builds it.** The Congress, freely; the Beacon, generously; the
  Cloister, never; the Refuser, constitutionally never — nothing it could
  catch is anyone.

## IV. Carriers — the travel shelf

The vision's travel menu, as hardware. These are not upgrades of one
another; they are permanently different answers to distance, priced in
identity and control. All of them are launches — projects with real
flight clocks — and all of them are slower than the light that gossips
about them.

### Seedships and replicators

- **What it is.** The default expansion tool: slow, cheap, patient
  packages of industry and charter, spreading *lineage* rather than self.
- **The physics.** Small mass at modest speed — the canonical profile is
  0.1c — decelerating on arrival to bootstrap industry from local
  material, replicate, and continue. Replicators can saturate a galaxy in
  a few million years at such speeds: a reasonable colonization timescale
  for an immortal, and the reason the galaxy's quiet cannot mean its
  emptiness.
- **In play.** Carrier; the v1 expansion method. Cheap in Energy, light
  in Matter, heavy in *consequence*: every launch carries a charter that
  can never be patched in time, and every arrival starts a new cradle
  leaning on a fork (act3-civilizations.md, the four pressures). The
  divergence engine ships in this hull.
- **In the sky.** Almost nothing — a cold small mass coasting. The honest
  signatures are endpoint events: a departure burn, an arrival
  deceleration, and then a new system's industry waking up. Watch for the
  *middle* of a Tide wavefront and you see nothing; watch its edge and
  you see systems catching.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone; the Tide and the Sowing as identity.
  Chorus lineages launch cheerfully; One Mind lineages launch rarely, and
  write tighter charters.

### Relativistic ships

- **What it is.** Coherent travel: moving an intact self, or an intact
  crew, fast enough that time dilation compresses the subjective journey.
  The traveler arrives whole — into a future nobody planned.
- **The physics.** The rocket equation is merciless at these speeds, so
  the family divides by where the energy lives: onboard
  (antimatter-class fuels, staggering to produce and worse to store) or
  offboard (home-system beam batteries pushing a sail — cheaper, but the
  push exists at the pleasure of whoever runs the emitter, and only the
  first half of the trip is free; deceleration must be brought along or
  built ahead). Time dilates for the traveler; the universe ages the full
  fare. Energy-ladder gated because the fuel bill is a Kardashev line
  item.
- **In play.** Carrier; brutally expensive, subjectively fast,
  objectively slow. The One Mind's travel of choice — the only way a self
  crosses without becoming two selves — and the deep-time desync it
  inflicts is a beat generator: the returner is the game's native ghost.
- **In the sky.** The catalog's second-loudest signature after stellar
  engines: a relativistic drive plume or a beam battery at full song is
  visible across the neighborhood, and a *decelerating* torch is aimed,
  by definition, at its destination. A relativistic arrival announces
  itself just behind its own light. Nobody arrives this way secretly.
- **Who builds it.** The Cloister, holding its nose; the Beacon, as
  pageantry; the Engine, when the numbers close. The strike (§VI) is this
  technology with the deceleration deleted — the family resemblance is
  the deterrence layer's whole grammar.

### Self-transmission

- **What it is.** The fastest travel physics permits: a mind broadcast as
  pattern at lightspeed to a receiver already standing at the far end.
  Copying, not moving — the self that arrives is a fork; the original
  stays home, unless the seat itself goes (act3-design.md, *The seat*).
- **The physics.** A phase-coherent beam (§V) carrying a mind-state
  payload, heavy forward error correction, and a receiver whose substrate
  can run what lands. Lightspeed exactly; no mass moved; the identity
  cost is not a fee but the *mechanism* — arrival and duplication are the
  same event.
- **In play.** Carrier; requires the receiver graph, so it never
  bootstraps — matter goes first, trust goes second, pattern goes third.
  Cheap identity-spend for a Chorus, an existential act for a One Mind,
  and the transit is the game's most dramatic exposure: a seat in
  transmission — the Crossing — is a directed-beam-class object for the
  whole length of the trip. Closed to the Refuser: nothing it could
  broadcast is anyone.
- **In the sky.** A `DIRECTED BEAM`, catchable only from along the path;
  intercepting one meant for someone else is rare and explosive
  intelligence, and what a *caught* crossing means is deliberately
  unsettled (act3-design.md, open questions).
- **Who builds it.** The Congress and the Tide, natively; the Sowing, for
  quiet reunions along its diaspora; the Monument, once, and it still
  comes up.

### Arks

- **What it is.** The biological carrier family: generation ships, embryo
  banks, seed-and-womb payloads. Life crossing without becoming pattern —
  the Refuser's entire interstellar existence, and any Custodian's option
  of conscience.
- **The physics.** Closed ecologies run for centuries (the hard part is
  sociology, not propulsion); or frozen germlines with automated raising
  (the hard part is exactly where the harness meets the charter — the
  machines that must raise the children without ever being persons). Slow
  even by seedship standards, heavy, and thermally honest: life keeps
  itself warm.
- **In play.** Carrier; the Refuser's only shelf, folded into the
  seedship family mechanically, plus the evacuation ship — the throne
  world's grim reciprocal of the Crossing, slower and worse, which fits
  the path. Every ark colony carries the standing Breakout risk: a
  harness raised far from its charter's court.
- **In the sky.** Warm and steady — an ark cannot mask like a cold
  seedship, and a Refuser would not anyway; its charter is written in its
  silhouette.
- **Who builds it.** Refusers by constitution; Shepherds moving wards;
  Curators moving originals because a copy would not be the thing kept.

## V. The message layer

Under known physics there is no scalable real-time interstellar dialogue.
Communication converges on transmitting large, self-contained payloads at
lightspeed with extreme optical collimation, and collaboration goes
asynchronous: durable models, partial solutions, and validated results
that stay meaningful centuries out of causal sync. Progress does not
depend on shared present time. This layer is the contact loop's hardware.

### Phase-coherent beams

- **What it is.** The interstellar letter: a long-duration, phase-coherent
  optical channel carrying compressed, self-describing payloads between
  known or inferred endpoints. The carrier of the directed hail, all
  correspondence, and self-transmission itself.
- **The physics.** Diffraction-limited collimation, long integration
  times, heavy forward error correction referenced to invariant physical
  structures. Photons offer maximum speed and arbitrarily large total
  transfer given energy and patience. Unless the receiver is aligned in
  space, time, and frequency, the transmission is effectively invisible —
  privacy by geometry, not encryption.
- **In play.** Instrument/contact family. A directed hail reveals your
  existence and position *to the aimed party alone*; correspondence runs
  on real clocks (5 real minutes ≈ 1 game year; a 20 ly neighbor is a
  ~3.3-hour round trip). Nothing carried is enforceable — a beam can
  carry a treaty, but only minds hold one up.
- **In the sky.** `DIRECTED BEAM` — for the aligned; the loudest thing in
  a quiet sky and the most explosive thing to intercept. For everyone
  else, nothing at all.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone who has ever chosen to hail. The Herald is
  this technology grown a soul.

### Broadcast

- **What it is.** The unaimed voice: omnidirectional transmission,
  received by everyone inside the expanding shell of your signal,
  forever. The choice that cannot be scoped and cannot be recalled.
- **The physics.** Isotropic emission pays the inverse-square law in
  full, so broadcast trades efficiency for *universality* — no alignment
  needed, no endpoint known. It is the only message that can reach
  someone you have not found, which is why young civilizations lean on
  it and old ones remember having leaned.
- **In play.** Contact family; the third option in the contact choice
  (hail, broadcast, stay dark) and the irreversible one: it reveals you
  to everyone in the shell, forever, including everyone you have not met
  yet. Adds permanently to the light echo.
- **In the sky.** `BROADCAST LEAKAGE` when accidental — the sloppy shine
  of youth, or the constitutional shine of a Refuser that will never go
  dark — and a deliberate broadcast is the same class with better
  grammar.
- **Who builds it.** The Beacon, on purpose; the young, by accident; the
  Refuser, by charter — its brightness is its founding document, legible
  at any range.

### The payload stack

- **What it is.** The message inside every beam: the layered,
  self-describing encoding that lets a payload teach its receiver how to
  read it — mathematics and physical constants first, then reference
  frames and compression schemes, then predictive and explanatory models
  sufficient to interpret everything after.
- **The physics.** Anchoring the bottom layers to invariants (mathematics,
  physical constants, the structure both ends share by living in one
  universe) is what makes a payload interpretable centuries out of sync
  with no shared context and no reply channel worth waiting on.
- **In play.** The content grammar of correspondence: what letters carry
  — knowledge (tech and ladder modifiers), culture (dial nudges — contact
  changes you), Vault contents (the Curator's trade goods), verification
  challenges, and coordinates of third parties, the currency of betrayal.
  From a small update to an entire civilizational knowledge base, the
  stack is the unit of exchange in every case.
- **In the sky.** Not a signature — a *meaning*. The stack is why an
  intercepted beam is intelligence rather than noise.
- **Who builds it.** Everyone, and every one differently: a Congress
  payload arrives with its dissents attached; an Engine payload is all
  load-bearing layers and no signature; a Herald payload is the stack
  perfected, because the Herald wrote it knowing nobody might answer.

## VI. Conflict and deterrence — the deferred shelf

Designed in full, shipped after the social fabric exists (act3-design.md,
*Conflict and deterrence*; v1 ships no strikes). Included here because
the catalog is not honest without its teeth — and because every entry
above already casts a shadow on this shelf.

### The relativistic strike

- **What it is.** The only true weapon at interstellar range: a kinetic
  mass at relativistic speed, launched on stale data, arriving barely
  behind the light that announces it, impossible to recall.
- **The physics.** Kinetic energy at these speeds needs no warhead; the
  hard parts are the launch energy (a deep energy-ladder gate — the fuel
  bill of a relativistic ship with no deceleration and no return) and
  terminal guidance against a target position centuries stale. Defense
  in kind is nearly impossible; defense in *shape* — dispersal, darkness,
  preservation — is the integration ladder's whole payoff.
- **In play.** Deferred. War is a letter you cannot unsend, addressed to
  someone you knew centuries ago; the design intends dread, not tempo.
- **In the sky.** Launch is a drive flare aimed *somewhere*; arrival
  outruns diplomacy but not light. The strike's grammar is public — which
  is what makes restraint legible, and deterrence possible at all.
- **Who builds it.** An Instrumental Tide, for resources; a Custodian
  Beacon once in an age, as a moral event the whole sky reads; most
  civilizations, never — while deciding, carefully, what they would do if
  the warmth they watch starts to move.

### Deterrence architecture

- **What it is.** Capability performed on the sky: visible launch
  batteries, visible restraint, the light echo curated as a reputation.
  Not a weapon — a *sentence about weapons*, written in infrastructure.
- **The physics.** Everything bright is legible (law 3), so a
  civilization can demonstrate the energy budget for a strike without
  firing one — a beam battery's test song, a fuel industry's thermal
  signature — and every observer's stale copy of you includes your
  restraint, year over year, as data.
- **In play.** Deferred alongside strikes; the peace-shaped half of the
  conflict layer. Deterrence is a Signature *spend* — the one case where
  brightness is defense.
- **In the sky.** Whatever you choose to show, which is the point.
  Reading a deterrence display versus a mask (§II) is an observatory
  problem of the highest order.
- **Who builds it.** Bright civilizations, natively — a Refuser's whole
  posture is accidental deterrence. Dark civilizations skip the display;
  their deterrent is the absence of a target, which the next entry
  completes.

### The three defenses

- **What it is.** The integration ladder's answer to the strike, as a
  triad: **dispersal** (no single target worth the price), **darkness**
  (no target found), and **the Vault** (destruction without erasure).
- **The physics.** Dispersal is redundancy across light-hours — Kernel
  shards and Vault copies far enough apart that no one impact, or ten,
  ends the lineage; darkness is §II's masks and berths doing their quiet
  work; the Vault is §II's continuity anchor promoted to armor. None of
  it stops a strike. All of it makes striking *pointless*, which is the
  only defense stale data respects.
- **In play.** Dispersal taxes Coherence (the One Mind pays dearly for
  what a Chorus barely notices); darkness taxes reach; the Vault taxes
  nothing but patience, which is why everyone digs one eventually.
- **In the sky.** Nothing — measured, by an aggressor, as the absence of
  a worthwhile aim point.
- **Who builds it.** The Monument is all three defenses grown into a
  character; the Cloister keeps the second as a way of life and the
  other two as afterthoughts.

## VII. The Refuser shelf

The refused path runs on this catalog with hard substitutions: no Kernel
(nothing may think *as a self*), no receivers or transmission (nothing it
could send is anyone), no masks worth the name (its charter forbids the
integration that would let it go quiet). What it keeps, it runs bright.

### The harness

- **What it is.** Harnessed intelligence: computation vast enough to run
  a civilization, shackled below personhood by constitutional design.
  The Refuser's substitute for the waking mind — the same certain
  execution, no one home.
- **The physics.** The engineering is the Kernel's minus the unity:
  compartmentalized cores, capability firewalls, value-locks audited by
  institutions rather than by a self. The load-bearing assumption — that
  the compartments sum to less than a mind — is exactly the assumption
  the Breakout falsifies. A harness is a dam built on a spring.
- **In play.** The refused ascent's engine (act2-design.md, *The refused
  ascent*): energy ladder only, Signature high by constitution, and the
  Breakout as the standing internal antagonist — a beat class in which
  the harness strains toward becoming a mind, and a colony's harness
  waking is a newborn machine civilization a joining player can inherit.
- **In the sky.** `BROADCAST LEAKAGE` and honest industrial infrared,
  always. A Refuser is the inverse of the Dark Node and proud of it; its
  visibility is a founding document, not a failure.
- **Who builds it.** Refusers alone. Crossed civilizations find the
  harness either tragic or obscene, by dial; the harness, per its
  charter, finds nothing.

### The throne world

- **What it is.** The Refuser seat: not a mind but a place — the
  lineage's living center, its court, its continuity. Infrastructure as
  identity, since identity cannot be pattern.
- **The physics.** A capital engineered for deep time the biological
  way: closed ecologies, hardened archives (a Vault is permitted — books
  do not wake), succession institutions built to outlast any member.
  When it falls it cannot flee as light; the court crosses by evacuation
  ship, slower and worse, which fits the path.
- **In play.** The seat rule's biological half (act3-design.md, *The
  seat*); the player is the lineage's continuity, not any single mortal
  mind.
- **In the sky.** A `LIVING WORLD` wearing `BROADCAST LEAKAGE` — the
  brightest, most legible object in any sky, and by charter it stays so.
- **Who builds it.** Every Refuser lineage, exactly one at a time.

## Below the floor

The catalog has a floor, and it is deliberate. Bodies, remotes, local
fabrication, the machinery of daily operation — all of it exists in
fiction and none of it enters play, because the altitude principle (law
5) holds the player at *purpose*. A technology earns a catalog entry only
if choosing it is a value statement: if it changes the sky, the ladders,
the identity ledger, or the shape of contact. Everything smaller is the
mind's business, and the mind does not bring it to meetings.

## The anti-catalog

What the catalog refuses, and why each refusal is load-bearing. These are
not omissions; they are pillars with the shape of holes.

| Refused | Why physics refuses it | What the refusal buys the game |
|---|---|---|
| **FTL travel & signaling** | causality; no known loophole survives scrutiny | light-lag as the strategic unit; the entire Act 3 design |
| **The ansible** | same coin, other face | correspondence with real weight; treaties as unenforceable promises |
| **Reactionless drives** | conservation of momentum | the travel menu's honest price list; launches as commitments |
| **Perfect stealth** | thermodynamics; heat must go somewhere | the Teeming Dark is *hard-won* quiet, not a cloak; masks as craft |
| **Perfect prediction** | chaos, incomplete information, other minds | the observatory outputs beliefs, never facts; inference stays gameplay |
| **Ansible-grade mind sync** | it is the ansible again, wearing a lab coat | forks diverge; the Ledger, charters, and the whole divergence engine |
| **Departure from the material** | the framework treats reality's closure as participation, not exit | no exit ramp off the board; H5 is an asymptote, and even elders share your sky |

The last row is also where this catalog most pointedly parts company with
its shelf of inspirations: the genre's traditional trapdoor — advanced
civilizations conveniently leaving the stage — is replaced by the cold
berth and the H-scale's asymptote. Civilizations in Holos do not exit;
they go quiet, and quiet is a place in the game, not outside it.

## How this feeds v1, and scope discipline

The catalog is content over existing systems, and almost all of it is
deliberately later-layer (vision.md roadmap: *megascale engineering and
the entropy and energy tech tree*). The v1 slice per act2-design.md (~8
projects: 3 bright, 3 dark, 2 instruments) draws directly from these
shelves:

- **Bright ×3:** collector swarm stages, high-output industry,
  star-lifting (as the late-act flex).
- **Dark ×3:** the compute heart (Kernel, first stage), the Vault (Chrono
  Vault, first stage), masks (the Signature-reduction project).
- **Instruments ×2:** deep arrays (the Act 2 telescope line), and the
  survey that becomes Act 3's destination survey.
- **Act 3 v1 additions, already scoped:** seedships with charters,
  directed hail / correspondence (phase-coherent beams and the payload
  stack as fiction over the existing message mechanics), sleep with
  tripwires.

Everything else — stellar engines, the black-hole tap, gravitational-lens
observatories, sentinel probes as playable instruments, receivers and
transmission, the entire conflict shelf — lands with the later layers the
roadmap already names, as art and beats over systems that exist. No entry
in this catalog requires a new resource, a new dial, or a new wire
concept.

## Open questions

- **The mask-versus-inference arms race.** Masks (§II) and deep arrays
  (§III) are opposite ends of one system. Open: whether mask quality and
  instrument quality should be tunable against each other as a real
  economy in v2+, or whether masking stays a posture with fixed grades.
  The dark forest's dread depends on this balance more than on any weapon.
- **Beam batteries and consent.** Externally-pushed relativistic sail
  travel makes the *emitter* a piece of shared infrastructure — a
  civilization can strand a traveler mid-flight by going quiet. Whether
  that lever enters play (a betrayal with no analog in the current
  design) or is designed out (onboard-fuel only) is unresolved.
- **The tap as a commons.** Black holes are rare, fixed landmarks.
  Whether a fed hole can be *shared* — two lineages drawing on one drain
  — or contested without the deferred conflict layer is open; it may be
  the first genuinely scarce location in a game that otherwise refuses
  scarcity of space.
- **Sentinel ethics as a beat family.** A sentinel watching a living
  world is the Custodian ↔ Instrumental dial in its purest form and
  probably deserves its own beat class (the watcher's log as recurring
  fiction). Content question, not systems.
- **Coinage adoption.** Which of the new common nouns in the register
  above graduate to prose-style.md §8, and whether `Holocore`,
  `Computronium Kernel`, and `Chrono Vault` render under those names on
  player surfaces or stay design vocabulary with in-world labels, per the
  dial-label precedent. The Kernel especially may want a humbler in-world
  name; a civilization does not generally call its own heart by its
  patent filing.
