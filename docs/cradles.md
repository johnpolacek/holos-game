# Cradles — the base catalog

*A working list of 40 starting worlds for Act 1 (Incubation). This is the
pool the game draws from when it hands a player their home planet.*

> Related design: [vision.md](./vision.md) (§ Act 1, the cradle concept),
> [gameplay-walkthrough.md](./gameplay-walkthrough.md) (§ Session zero, § Act 1),
> [act2-design.md](./act2-design.md) (§ Derivation — how the cradle sets the dials).

---

## What a cradle is

Every player begins on a single world, and that world is not chosen from a
menu of comfortable Earths. It is generated from **real exoplanet statistics**,
so the odds favor the world-types the galaxy actually makes in bulk —
tidally-locked terminator worlds around red dwarfs, high-gravity super-Earths,
metal-poor ocean worlds, cold-edge deserts — not the rare blue marble we
happened to evolve on. Per the vision, a cradle is *either modeled on a known
world or extrapolated from occurrence-rate data*, and this catalog does both:
roughly half the entries are real, named exoplanets; the rest are archetypes
extrapolated from world-types we know are common.

The cradle does three jobs across the whole game:

1. **It sets Act 1 difficulty.** Harsher worlds hand out harsher odds —
   narrower windows, crueler failures on the dice (gameplay-walkthrough.md,
   *"Difficulty is the cradle"*).
2. **It sets the base of the Act 2 dials.** At the singularity the mind wakes
   with a character sheet of five dials, and the cradle sets each dial's
   **base position and range width**. Harsh worlds *narrow* the ranges — a
   civilization that clawed through a terminator strip or a crushing gravity
   well wakes with firmer convictions and less room to move (act2-design.md,
   *"Derivation"*).
3. **It leaves a fingerprint on the fiction.** The same pressure that shaped
   what survived shapes what the survivors built, and eventually what the
   superintelligence they become actually wants.

This list is a **base** — 40 cradles is enough to prove the variety pass v1
calls for and to give the Act-1-to-dials mapping something concrete to bind to.
It is meant to grow.

## The five dials (shorthand)

Every entry ends with a **dial lean**: the direction the cradle pushes each
dial before the player has taken a single Act 1 branch. Full dial design lives
in act2-design.md; the shorthand here is:

| Dial | Ends |
|---|---|
| **Reach ↔ Depth** | spend outward (Reach) vs. inward (Depth) |
| **Voice ↔ Silence** | wants to be heard (Voice) vs. wants to hide (Silence) |
| **Custodian ↔ Instrumental** | other life is precious (Custodian) vs. material (Instrumental) |
| **One Mind ↔ Chorus** | one coherent self (One Mind) vs. many diverging selves (Chorus) |
| **Curator ↔ Shedder** | the past is worth keeping (Curator) vs. worth shedding (Shedder) |

**How the cradle leans each dial** (the design logic, applied consistently
below so the mapping is legible rather than arbitrary):

- **Reach ↔ Depth** — room and resources push *Reach*: bright stars, wide
  systems, easy launch. Confinement and scarcity push *Depth*: deep gravity
  wells, cramped habitable bands, drowned or buried biospheres.
- **Voice ↔ Silence** — a hostile sky pushes *Silence*: flare stars, heavy
  bombardment, worlds where being noticed meant being hurt. A calm, legible
  sky pushes *Voice*: clear air, a rich starfield, stable stars.
- **Custodian ↔ Instrumental** — abundance pushes *Custodian*: a lush, forgiving
  biosphere teaches that life is to be kept. Scarcity pushes *Instrumental*:
  a world where survival meant using everything teaches that life is material.
- **One Mind ↔ Chorus** — a connected, uniform environment pushes *One Mind*:
  a global ocean, a single terminator ring. A fragmented one pushes *Chorus*:
  islands, isolated seas, patchy refuges that raised many separate lineages.
- **Curator ↔ Shedder** — deep, stable, continuous history pushes *Curator*:
  ancient quiet worlds with long memory. Repeated catastrophe pushes *Shedder*:
  volatile worlds where survival meant leaping the gap and leaving the past
  behind.

**Range width** is set by difficulty: gentle cradles leave *wide* ranges (the
player's Act 1 choices can move the dials far); brutal cradles leave *narrow*
ranges (the world's verdict is close to final).

## Difficulty tiers

| Tier | Name | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | Gentle | forgiving beats, generous rolls; the onboarding cradles. Rare in the pool. |
| **II** | Temperate | a fair climb with real setbacks. |
| **III** | Testing | narrow windows; the statistically-typical world. |
| **IV** | Harsh | crushing pressure on every beat; failure is the default. |
| **V** | Brutal | life barely holds; most histories here are short. For veterans. |

**Spawn weight** reflects how common the world-type is under real occurrence
rates, so the pool skews toward Tiers III–IV. Legend: **Common / Uncommon /
Rare / Very rare.** (Earth-twins are *Very rare* by design.)

## The catalog

Entries are grouped by host-star population, which is the strongest driver of
what worlds actually exist: M dwarfs are ~70% of all stars, so they dominate
the pool. Within each group, real named worlds come first, then extrapolated
archetypes.

---

### M dwarfs — the red-dwarf majority

*Small, cool, long-lived, and everywhere. Habitable zones sit close in, so
worlds are usually tidally locked; many hosts flare. This is where most
cradles come from.*

#### 1. TRAPPIST-1e
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed, one of the best-studied temperate worlds.
- **Host star:** TRAPPIST-1, an ultracool M8 dwarf; seven packed terrestrial planets.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate insolation · likely water · atmosphere unknown · tidally locked (terminator world) · very old system.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** A genuine terminator strip: life crowds the ring of endless dusk between a frozen night side and a baked day side.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization raised in a thin livable band, sky owned by a huge dim sun that never moves and six sister worlds hanging close. Astronomy is native here — the neighbors are *right there*.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (cramped band), **Voice** (an unmissable, legible sky full of close worlds), **One Mind** (a single connected ring). Moderate ranges.

#### 2. TRAPPIST-1f
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; cooler outer sibling of 1e.
- **Host star:** TRAPPIST-1 (as above).
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · cool insolation · likely a deep ocean under ice · tidally locked · very old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Colder than 1e; open water may exist only over the substellar point, ringed by ice.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** An eyeball ocean — a warm meltwater pupil facing the sun, an ice shell everywhere else. Life pools where the light lands and freezes out of reach beyond it.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, **One Mind** (one central sea), lean **Curator** (a slow, stable, ancient world). Narrowish ranges.

#### 3. Proxima Centauri b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; the nearest exoplanet to Earth.
- **Host star:** Proxima Centauri, an M5.5 flare star (part of the Alpha Centauri triple).
- **Profile:** ~Earth-plus gravity · temperate insolation · water uncertain · heavy XUV/flare bombardment · tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** The star flares violently; the day side is periodically scoured by radiation. Any biosphere learns to hide.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** Life under a dangerous sky, sheltering from flares in caves, deep water, or the terminator's shadow. The lesson written into everything is: *do not be exposed.*
- **Dial lean:** **Silence** (a sky that hurts), **Depth** (shelter over sprawl), lean **Shedder** (repeated flare catastrophes cull the past). Narrow ranges.

#### 4. LHS 1140 b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; a dense, well-characterized super-Earth.
- **Host star:** LHS 1140, a quiet M4.5 dwarf (low flare activity).
- **Profile:** high gravity (~1.5–2 g) · temperate insolation · dense rock, possibly a water-world or with a deep ocean · thick atmosphere plausible · tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Heavy gravity flattens everything, but the star is calm — a harsh world under a gentle sun.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A crushing, high-pressure world where nothing stands tall and orbit is a nearly impossible height to climb out of. Ambition here is heavy, literally.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (a deep gravity well; the sky is expensive to reach), lean **One Mind**, neutral Voice. Moderate ranges.

#### 5. Ross 128 b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed.
- **Host star:** Ross 128, an unusually **quiet** M4 dwarf (rare low-flare red dwarf).
- **Profile:** ~Earth-plus gravity · temperate insolation · water plausible · calm radiation environment · likely tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **II (Temperate).** One of the kindest red-dwarf cradles: temperate, and the star doesn't try to kill you.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A red-dwarf world that got lucky — steady light, a survivable sky, time to develop without constant catastrophe. A gentle on-ramp that still isn't Earth.
- **Dial lean:** **Voice** (a calm, readable sky), lean **Custodian** (a forgiving world), neutral Reach/Depth. Wide ranges.

#### 6. TOI-700 d
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; TESS's first temperate Earth-size HZ world.
- **Host star:** TOI-700, a quiet M2 dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate insolation · likely ocean-bearing · low flare activity · tidally locked · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Temperate and calm-starred, but a locked ocean world with all the terminator constraints.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A mild waterworld under a steady red sun — global seas, weather driven by the eternal day-night contrast, life following the currents between hot and cold hemispheres.
- **Dial lean:** **One Mind** (a global connected ocean), neutral Reach/Depth, lean **Voice**. Moderate–wide ranges.

#### 7. Teegarden's Star b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed.
- **Host star:** Teegarden's Star, an old, quiet M7 ultracool dwarf, very nearby.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate insolation · water plausible · low activity · tidally locked · very old (~8 Gyr).
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Habitable but dim — the sun is barely a coal; photosynthesis is a scarce, prized trick.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** An ancient world under a faint, ancient star. Deep time, little energy — a civilization that grows slow, patient, and thrifty with light.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, **Curator** (immense age, deep memory), lean **Silence** (a dim, unassuming world). Moderate ranges.

#### 8. GJ 667 Cc
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed super-Earth.
- **Host star:** GJ 667 C, an M1.5 dwarf in a **triple-star system** (orbits alongside a K/K binary).
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.3–1.5 g) · temperate insolation · water plausible · tidally locked · old, metal-poor system.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** A heavier world, but the standout is the sky: a close red sun plus two bright far stars that never fully set.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A world with three suns and no true darkness. Astronomy and mythology are dominated by a sky that is never empty; a civilization that always knew it was not alone in the heavens.
- **Dial lean:** **Voice** (a spectacular, others-implying sky), lean **Reach** (a rich multi-star system to grow into), neutral Custodian. Moderate ranges.

#### 9. Kepler-186f
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; first Earth-size world in an M-dwarf HZ.
- **Host star:** Kepler-186, an M1 dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · **cold** outer-edge insolation · ice-prone surface · atmosphere unknown · likely slow/locked rotation · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Sits at the cold margin — a snowball unless the atmosphere is thick. Habitability hangs on greenhouse luck.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A cold-edge world clinging to the far habitable rim, life concentrated around geothermal warmth and equatorial thaw. Scarcity of heat is the organizing pressure.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, lean **Instrumental** (a stingy world; everything is used), **One Mind**. Narrowish ranges.

#### 10. Gliese 12 b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed (2024); temperate Earth-size.
- **Host star:** Gliese 12, a cool, relatively quiet M dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate-to-warm insolation (Venus-or-Earth boundary) · atmosphere retention uncertain · likely tidally locked · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Right at the inner edge — could be temperate, could be sliding toward runaway warmth. A world on a knife.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A warm terminator world where the day side runs hot and the habitable margin is a moving line. Life tracks the shifting band where water stays liquid.
- **Dial lean:** neutral Reach/Depth, lean **Voice**, lean **Shedder** (a climate that periodically resets). Moderate ranges.

#### 11. Luyten b (GJ 273 b)
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; a nearby temperate super-Earth.
- **Host star:** Luyten's Star, a quiet M3.5 dwarf.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.4 g) · temperate insolation · water plausible · low activity · tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Heavier than Earth, temperate, calm-starred — a solid mid-tier red-dwarf climb.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A steady, weighty world with a stable sky — no single savage pressure, just the constant heaviness and the fixed sun. Endurance over drama.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (weight), **One Mind**, neutral elsewhere. Moderate–wide ranges.

#### 12. K2-18 b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; a leading hycean-world candidate.
- **Host star:** K2-18, an M2.5 dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~8.6 M⊕ mini-Neptune · low surface gravity at cloud-top · possible global ocean under a **hydrogen atmosphere** · warm · old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** A hycean world: life (if any) lives in a deep ocean beneath a thick H₂ envelope, with no land, no fire, no metallurgy, and no view of the stars.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A drowned civilization under a hydrogen sky it can never see through. Technology has to skip fire entirely; the path to space runs through an ocean and an atmosphere that both fight you.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (buried under ocean and sky), **Silence** (blind to the cosmos, unseen by it), **One Mind** (one global sea). Narrow ranges.

#### 13. Terminator storm world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a common outcome for locked M-dwarf worlds with thick atmospheres.
- **Host star:** M dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth-plus gravity · temperate mean, savage gradients · water present · thick atmosphere · tidally locked · variable.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** The day-night temperature contrast drives permanent hurricane-scale winds along the terminator. Life survives in the lee of terrain.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A world of endless storms, where the only calm is the shelter of mountains and the deep. A civilization built low, braced, and wind-wise, reading weather as its oldest science.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, lean **Silence** (a battered, hunkered world), **Shedder** (storms erase; you learn to let go). Narrow ranges.

#### 14. Eyeball ice world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — the cold-side outcome of a locked M-dwarf ocean world.
- **Host star:** M dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · cold mean · a substellar meltpool in an ice shell · thin-to-moderate atmosphere · tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Liquid water exists only in a warm eye facing the sun; everything else is a glacier. Habitat is a single bright puddle in a frozen world.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** All life in one sunlit eye of open water, hemmed by ice on every side. A concentrated, crowded origin with a hard edge and nowhere to spread.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, **One Mind** (one habitable eye), lean **Instrumental** (scarcity). Narrow ranges.

#### 15. Tidally-heated moon
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a habitable moon of a gas giant in an M-dwarf system, warmed by tidal flexing (Io/Europa-adjacent, but livable).
- **Host star:** M dwarf (life powered as much by tides as by light).
- **Profile:** ~Earth-minus gravity · warmth from **internal tidal heating**, not the star · water and volcanism both abundant · thin atmosphere · phase-locked to its giant · young-feeling, resurfaced often.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Geologically violent — constant quakes and eruptions — but energy-rich from below. A world lit from within.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** Life around volcanic vents and warm cracks, indifferent to the dim sun, keyed to the giant that fills half the sky and the tides that knead the crust. Chemistry, not sunlight, is the source of everything.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Reach** (energy-rich, a giant and its moons to grow into), **Shedder** (constant resurfacing erases the past), lean **Voice**. Moderate ranges.

#### 16. Metal-poor drowned world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — the vision's flagship *"metal-poor ocean world with no continents."*
- **Host star:** M or K dwarf, metal-poor.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate · a deep **global ocean** floored by high-pressure ice, not rock · thin atmosphere · locked or slow rotation · old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** No dry land, and the seafloor is ice, not stone — so almost no dissolved metals or minerals. A biosphere starved of the elements technology is built from.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** An entirely aquatic, mineral-poor civilization that never had rock, fire, or metal within reach. The road to technology has to be invented from biology and water alone, if it can be walked at all.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (drowned and buried), **One Mind** (one ocean), **Instrumental** (radical scarcity teaches ruthless use of everything). Narrow ranges.

### K dwarfs — the orange "Goldilocks" stars

*Cooler and steadier than the Sun, longer-lived, less flare-prone than M
dwarfs. Often called the best real estate for life: wide, stable habitable
zones with room outside the tidal-lock trap. Rarer than M dwarfs, still common.*

#### 17. Kepler-442b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; scores near the top of habitability indices.
- **Host star:** Kepler-442, a K5 dwarf.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.3 g) · temperate insolation · water plausible · possibly slow (not fully locked) rotation · old, calm star.
- **Difficulty:** **II (Temperate).** Heavier than Earth but bathed in steady orange light — one of the most forgiving non-Earth cradles known.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A stable, weighty, well-lit world with day and night and seasons — close enough to Earth's rhythm to develop broadly, heavy enough to remember it's not Earth.
- **Dial lean:** near-neutral all dials; faint **Custodian** and **Voice**. Wide ranges (a gentle world leaves the mind free).

#### 18. Kepler-62f
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; a leading water-world candidate.
- **Host star:** Kepler-62, a K2 dwarf.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity · cool outer-HZ insolation · likely a global ocean · thick atmosphere needed for warmth · rotation uncertain · old.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** A cool ocean world at the outer edge — habitable if the greenhouse holds, glaciated if it doesn't.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A deep, cool sea-world with little or no land, life spread through a single planetary ocean, weather and ice defining the livable latitudes.
- **Dial lean:** **One Mind** (global ocean), lean **Depth**, lean **Curator** (a stable, slow world). Moderate–wide ranges.

#### 19. HD 40307 g
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed super-Earth in a multi-planet system.
- **Host star:** HD 40307, a quiet K2.5 dwarf.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.7 g) · temperate insolation · water plausible · a real day-night cycle (wider orbit, not locked) · old.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Heavy but well-placed and free-rotating — the gravity is the main antagonist, not the star or the lock.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A rotating super-Earth with proper days, seasons, and continents under heavy gravity. Life is low-slung and strong; culture develops with a normal sky-clock but a heavy tread.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (gravity well), neutral Voice, lean **Custodian**. Moderate–wide ranges.

#### 20. 40 Eridani A b
- **Basis:** Real — **candidate / disputed** (the "Vulcan" signal; some analyses attribute it to stellar activity).
- **Host star:** 40 Eridani A, a K0.5 dwarf in a triple system (with a white dwarf and a red dwarf companion far out).
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity · temperate insolation · water plausible · rotating · old, sun-like-but-cooler.
- **Difficulty:** **II (Temperate).** A near-ideal orange-dwarf world *if* it's real — steady light, gentle enough for a long climb.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare (flagged as candidate).
- **Fingerprint:** A temperate world with a distant, uncanny pair of stellar companions — a nearby white dwarf that once was a sun, a permanent reminder in the sky that stars die.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Custodian**, lean **Curator** (a sky that literally shows mortality), neutral Reach/Depth. Wide ranges.

#### 21. Cold-edge desert world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — the vision's flagship *"cold-edge desert clinging to the far habitable margin."*
- **Host star:** K or M dwarf, world near the outer HZ.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · cold, dry insolation · very little surface water (locked in ice caps and deep aquifers) · thin, dry atmosphere · rotating or slow · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Arid and cold — an Arrakis at the frost line. Water is the scarce, sacred resource around which everything organizes.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A desert civilization for whom every drop counts, life clustered at ice margins and geothermal springs, a culture of conservation and hoarding baked in from the first cell.
- **Dial lean:** **Instrumental** (brutal scarcity), lean **Depth**, lean **Curator** (nothing is wasted, including memory). Narrowish ranges.

#### 22. The temperate twin
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — the deliberately rare near-Earth cradle. The onboarding world.
- **Host star:** K or G dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate insolation · abundant surface water and land · breathable-track atmosphere · normal day-night rotation · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **I (Gentle).** The blue marble the galaxy rarely makes. Forgiving beats, generous rolls — the tutorial cradle you earn your way off of.
- **Spawn weight:** Very rare.
- **Fingerprint:** A balanced, generous world with room to spread, land and sea both, a legible sky. Life diversifies freely; the civilization inherits abundance and options rather than a single defining wound.
- **Dial lean:** near-neutral on all dials by design. **Widest** ranges in the pool — this cradle constrains the mind least, so Act 1 choices matter most.

### G dwarfs — sun-like stars

*Our own kind of star: bright, warm, but shorter-lived and rarer than the cool
dwarfs. Wide habitable zones, but the clock runs faster and the star brightens
with age. Uncommon in the pool.*

#### 23. Kepler-452b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed (with some vetting caveats); "Earth's older cousin."
- **Host star:** Kepler-452, a G2 star ~1.5 Gyr older than the Sun.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.6 g) · warm, **rising** insolation (an aging, brightening sun) · water plausible but evaporating · rotating · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** An old Earth under an aging sun — entering a moist-greenhouse phase, the oceans beginning to boil away. A world with a deadline.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization racing a warming sun, a biosphere that peaked and is now being cooked from above. Time pressure is written into the sky; the future is visibly finite.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Reach** (get off before it cooks), **Voice**, lean **Shedder** (a past being burned away). Moderate ranges.

#### 24. Kepler-22b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; a classic possible ocean world.
- **Host star:** Kepler-22, a G5 star slightly cooler than the Sun.
- **Profile:** ~2.4 R⊕, likely low-density · temperate insolation · plausibly a **global ocean** or ocean-over-ice · thick atmosphere · rotating · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Sun-like light on a probable waterworld — mild climate, but little or no land to stand on.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A warm world-ocean under a familiar yellow sun, seasons and days intact but continents absent. An aquatic civilization with a clear view of the stars — rare, and important.
- **Dial lean:** **One Mind** (global ocean), **Voice** (clear sky, visible cosmos), neutral Reach/Depth. Moderate–wide ranges.

#### 25. Tau Ceti f
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed candidate in a nearby, well-studied system.
- **Host star:** Tau Ceti, a G8 star notable for being **metal-poor** and stable, orbited by a thick debris disk.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity · cool outer-HZ insolation · water plausible · heavy comet/asteroid bombardment (thick debris disk) · rotating · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Sun-like and rotating, but the metal-poor system means fewer heavy elements to build with, and the debris disk means frequent impacts.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A world under regular bombardment from its own crowded system, its history punctuated by impacts, its crust metal-lean. A civilization shaped by catastrophe from the sky and scarcity in the ground.
- **Dial lean:** **Silence** (a dangerous sky teaches wariness of what's out there), **Shedder** (repeated impacts reset the board), lean **Instrumental**. Narrowish ranges.

#### 26. 55 Cancri e
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed; an extreme, well-characterized lava world.
- **Host star:** 55 Cancri A, a G8 star (naked-eye, metal-rich, five known planets).
- **Profile:** ~8 M⊕ super-Earth · **molten day side** (~2000 K) · possibly carbon-rich (graphite/diamond interior) · no water · tidally locked · old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** Not surface-habitable at all. A cradle here can only be a deep, shielded, high-temperature-chemistry biosphere — an extreme, edge-of-viable start.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare (extreme edge case).
- **Fingerprint:** Life, if it exists, is subsurface and heat-loving, in a carbon-rich crust under a sky of rock vapor. A civilization forged in fire and carbon, alien in its chemistry and its relationship to the surface it can never inhabit.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (buried by necessity), **Silence**, **Shedder** (a merciless world). Narrowest ranges — the world's verdict is nearly final.

#### 27. Runaway-greenhouse-edge world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a Venus-adjacent world holding at the survivable inner margin.
- **Host star:** G or K dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth-plus gravity · hot, high insolation · water only at poles / high altitude · thick CO₂ atmosphere · slow rotation · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** A hot, high-pressure world one nudge from runaway greenhouse. Life clings to the cool margins — poles, peaks, night.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization of the cool corners, heat-fleeing, altitude- and pole-dwelling, its whole history a negotiation with a suffocating sky. Climate is the ever-present antagonist.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Reach** (escape the oven), **Instrumental** (harsh scarcity of livable space), lean **Shedder**. Narrowish ranges.

### F dwarfs — the bright, fast-burning stars

*Hotter and more luminous than the Sun, with intense UV and short main-sequence
lifetimes. Wide, distant habitable zones under a harsh ultraviolet sky, and a
faster evolutionary clock. Rare.*

#### 28. UV-scoured F-star world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a terrestrial world in an F star's distant HZ.
- **Host star:** F-type star (bright, hot, high-UV, short-lived).
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · high visible + **UV** insolation · water present · thick ozone/haze shield required · rotating · geologically young (the star hasn't lived long).
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Fierce UV sterilizes exposed surfaces; life needs a thick shield or deep water, and it has less time than usual to get anywhere.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization that evolved under a burning sky, driven to shade, ozone, and water, racing a star that will die young. Pressure comes from above and from the clock at once.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Reach** (a short window forces ambition), **Silence** (the sky is a threat), lean **Shedder**. Moderate ranges.

#### 29. Bright super-Earth
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a resource-rich heavy world under an F or bright-G star.
- **Host star:** F/early-G star, metal-rich.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~1.6 g) · bright, warm insolation · abundant water and heavy elements · thick atmosphere · rotating · young-to-middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **III (Testing).** Heavy and UV-bright, but rich — plenty of energy, water, and metal to work with once you survive the surface.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare.
- **Fingerprint:** A world of abundance under a demanding sun — heavy, bright, mineral-rich. A civilization that grows expansive and energetic, used to having enough, racing the star's shorter life.
- **Dial lean:** **Reach** (energy and resource abundance; a short clock), **Voice**, lean **Custodian** (abundance breeds generosity). Wide ranges.

### Exotic, ancient, and compositional cradles

*Worlds defined by their history or their chemistry rather than their star:
ancient metal-poor relics, circumbinary worlds, and the odd compositions —
iron, carbon, ice, haze — that occurrence data says exist in quantity.*

#### 30. Kapteyn b
- **Basis:** Real — **disputed** (the signal has been challenged as stellar activity), but a real and important *archetype*: the ancient halo world.
- **Host star:** Kapteyn's Star, an M1 subdwarf from the galactic **halo** — one of the oldest stars near the Sun (~11 Gyr), extremely metal-poor.
- **Profile:** super-Earth gravity (~4.8 M⊕) · temperate insolation · water plausible · likely locked · **ancient**.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Metal-poor to the bone and unimaginably old — a world where the elements of technology are scarce and time is effectively unlimited.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare (flagged as disputed).
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization on a relic world older than most of the galaxy's stars, patient beyond measure, mineral-starved, its history reaching back into deep cosmic time. Age is the defining fact.
- **Dial lean:** **Curator** (deep, deep memory), **Depth**, lean **Instrumental** (metal scarcity). Narrowish ranges.

#### 31. Barnard's Star b
- **Basis:** Real — confirmed (2024); a sub-Earth, not habitable itself, included as the *cold ancient-dwarf* archetype.
- **Host star:** Barnard's Star, an old, metal-poor, quiet M4 dwarf — the closest single star to the Sun.
- **Profile:** sub-Earth gravity · cold, low insolation (too hot at its true orbit, but the archetype is the cold outer sibling) · thin atmosphere · locked · very old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** A small, cold, metal-poor world under a faint ancient star — thin on gravity, warmth, and material all at once.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A frigid, low-gravity relic where life scrapes by on geothermal warmth and thin air. A civilization of the cold and the ancient, small and enduring, holding on rather than reaching out.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, **Silence**, **Curator**. Narrow ranges.

#### 32. Circumbinary world (Kepler-16 type)
- **Basis:** Real host (Kepler-16 is a confirmed circumbinary system) — the cradle is an extrapolated habitable world/moon with two suns.
- **Host star:** A close **binary** (e.g. a K + M pair); the world orbits both.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate but **variable** insolation (two suns, shifting distances and eclipses) · water plausible · rotating · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Two suns mean complex, non-repeating seasons and irregular light and heat — a climate that never settles into a simple year.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A Tatooine — two suns rising and setting out of step, seasons that don't repeat, life adapted to irregularity itself. A civilization whose sky taught it that the heavens are plural and unpredictable.
- **Dial lean:** **Voice** (a sky that always implied more than one of everything), lean **Chorus** (variability favored many strategies, not one), neutral Reach/Depth. Moderate ranges.

#### 33. Super-Mercury (iron world)
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a dense, iron-rich world; common around metal-rich stars, and produced by mantle-stripping impacts.
- **Host star:** Any; often metal-rich G/K.
- **Profile:** very high gravity · warm-to-hot insolation · little water · thin atmosphere · slow or locked rotation · variable age.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** A stripped, iron-dominated world — heavy, dry, and mineral-lopsided (metal everywhere, light elements scarce). A crushing, barren start.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization on a great ball of iron and rock — abundant metal, scarce water and air, a savage gravity well. Metallurgy is native and effortless; everything else is a fight.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (deep well, hard to leave), lean **Instrumental** (a world you mine to live), **Silence**. Narrow ranges.

#### 34. Carbon world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a carbon-dominated planet (graphite crust, diamond mantle); forms around carbon-rich stars.
- **Host star:** A carbon-rich star (C/O ratio > 1).
- **Profile:** ~Earth-plus gravity · warm insolation · **tar/hydrocarbon** seas rather than water, or dry graphite plains · reducing atmosphere · rotating · variable age.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** A world of carbon chemistry with little free water and little oxygen — life, if it arises, runs on utterly different chemistry.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization of carbon and hydrocarbons, diamonds underfoot and tar in the low places, oxygen a rarity. Its biology and its technology both start from a chemistry alien to ours.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Depth**, **Shedder**, neutral Voice. Moderate ranges.

#### 35. Coreless silicate world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a low-density world that never differentiated a metal core (little iron, no strong magnetic field).
- **Host star:** M or K dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate insolation · water plausible · **no global magnetic field** (weak protection from stellar wind and radiation) · rotating · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Without a magnetic shield, the surface is exposed to stellar radiation and the atmosphere slowly bleeds to space. Life stays sheltered or underwater.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization under an unshielded sky, sheltering from radiation, its air thinning over eons. Metal-poor and exposed, it learns early that the surface is dangerous and space is worse.
- **Dial lean:** **Silence**, **Depth**, lean **Instrumental**. Narrowish ranges.

#### 36. Subsurface-ocean ice world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a Europa/Enceladus-type world with life in a **buried ocean** under an ice shell, warmed from below.
- **Host star:** Any; often a moon far from a dim star, heated by tides or radionuclides.
- **Profile:** ~Earth-minus gravity · frozen surface, liquid ocean beneath kilometers of ice · no atmosphere to speak of · vent-driven chemistry · very old.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** Life lives sealed under ice, blind to the sky, powered by hydrothermal vents. The road to technology has no fire, no view of space, and a literal ceiling of ice.
- **Spawn weight:** Common (subsurface oceans may be the galaxy's most abundant habitat).
- **Fingerprint:** A sealed civilization that grew in total darkness around vents, that has to *discover* the sky exists before it can dream of reaching it. Everything is inward until the ice is breached.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (sealed and buried), **Silence** (unseeing and unseen), **One Mind** (one enclosed ocean). Narrow ranges.

#### 37. Titan-analog haze world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a cold world with **hydrocarbon** seas and a thick organic haze (a Titan grown into a cradle).
- **Host star:** Any; a cold outer-system world with internal or greenhouse warmth.
- **Profile:** low gravity · very cold · **methane/ethane** seas, water-ice bedrock · thick nitrogen-hydrocarbon atmosphere, orange haze · slow rotation · old.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Cryogenic chemistry — life (if any) runs on liquid hydrocarbons, not water. Slow, cold, and utterly unlike Earth.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization of the deep cold, breathing haze, sailing methane seas, its chemistry glacially slow and its sky a permanent orange murk. Patience and cold are its native conditions.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth**, **Silence** (a hazed-over sky, never seeing out), lean **Curator**. Moderate ranges.

#### 38. High-obliquity world
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a world with extreme axial tilt (violent, planet-spanning seasons).
- **Host star:** G or K dwarf.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · temperate mean, **extreme seasonal swings** (poles that bake then freeze) · water present · rotating with a large tilt · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Each hemisphere roasts and freezes in turn; life must migrate, hibernate, or endure both extremes. Stability is the one thing the climate never offers.
- **Spawn weight:** Uncommon.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization built on migration and cycle, keyed to violent seasons, its cultures nomadic and its biology hardened to extremes. Change, not stability, is the baseline it evolved to expect.
- **Dial lean:** lean **Chorus** (many niches, migratory strategies, no single answer), **Shedder** (seasons demand letting go), neutral Reach/Depth. Moderate ranges.

#### 39. Rogue / geothermal world (Stevenson planet)
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — a **starless** world (ejected or free-floating) kept warm by internal heat under a thick hydrogen blanket.
- **Host star:** None. Warmth is entirely geothermal.
- **Profile:** ~Earth gravity · no sunlight, warm surface from below · liquid-water seas under a thick insulating atmosphere · perpetual darkness · ancient.
- **Difficulty:** **V (Brutal).** No sun at all — no photosynthesis, no seasons, no sky. Life runs entirely on geothermal chemistry in eternal night.
- **Spawn weight:** Rare.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization that arose in total, permanent darkness with no concept of a sun, no stars, no day. When it finally pierces its atmosphere it discovers a universe it had no reason to imagine — a first-contact with the cosmos itself.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (no outward frame at all), **Silence** (a world that never knew it could be seen), **Curator** (a slow, unbroken, sunless history). Narrow ranges.

#### 40. Crushing super-Earth
- **Basis:** Extrapolated — the vision's flagship *"super-Earth whose heavy gravity flattens everything."* The pure high-gravity archetype.
- **Host star:** K or G dwarf (so the star isn't the problem — the gravity is).
- **Profile:** very high gravity (~2–3 g) · temperate insolation · abundant water and land · thick, dense atmosphere · rotating · middle-aged.
- **Difficulty:** **IV (Harsh).** Nothing stands tall; everything is low, broad, and strong. The atmosphere is thick, the horizon is close, and orbit is a mountain nearly too high to climb.
- **Spawn weight:** Common.
- **Fingerprint:** A civilization built low and heavy, flight and spaceflight brutally expensive, the sky a ceiling more than an invitation. It masters its surface long before it can even dream of leaving it — and leaving is the hardest thing it will ever do.
- **Dial lean:** **Depth** (a gravity well that fights every ambition to reach out), lean **One Mind**, lean **Custodian** (a rich, forgiving surface). Moderate ranges.

## Notes and open questions

- **This is a base, not the final set.** 40 is enough to prove the variety pass
  and to give the Act-1-to-dials mapping (act2-design.md, still open) something
  concrete to bind against. Adding cradles later is cheap and additive.
- **Spawn weighting vs. difficulty selection.** Two dials interact: real
  occurrence rates (which make harsh worlds common) and the player's optional
  difficulty setting (gameplay-walkthrough.md). Open question: does the
  difficulty dial *bias the draw* toward a tier, or *overlay* a modifier on
  whatever cradle is drawn? The vision implies the latter (cradle first, dial on
  top), but a first-run onboarding path should probably guarantee a Tier I–II
  draw regardless.
- **Real vs. disputed worlds.** Several real anchors (40 Eridani A b / "Vulcan",
  Kapteyn b) are contested in the literature. They earn their place as
  *archetypes* even if the specific detection doesn't survive; the flag is kept
  honest in the Basis column so the fiction never overclaims the science.
- **Dial derivation is a lean, not a verdict.** Each entry's dial lean is only
  the *base* the cradle sets. Branches taken and rolls survived in Act 1 move
  the positions inside the ranges (act2-design.md, *"Derivation"*). The narrow
  ranges on the brutal cradles are the mechanical teeth of *"harsh worlds narrow
  ranges."*
- **Data shape (for when this leaves Markdown).** Each cradle already has the
  fields a generator needs: host-star class, a physical profile
  (gravity / insolation / water / atmosphere / metallicity / rotation / age), a
  difficulty tier, a spawn weight, and a per-dial base+range lean. When Act 1 is
  built, this table is the natural seed for a typed `Cradle` record in the
  protocol.
