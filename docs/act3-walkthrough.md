# HOLOS
### Act 3, Played — one player's first season in the dark

*A detailed, hour-by-hour account of a specific player's first six weeks of
Act 3: one civilization, one sky, one season of real time. Where
[gameplay-walkthrough.md](./gameplay-walkthrough.md) describes the designed
experience in general second person, this document runs it — with a named
player, a played history, and the clock math done honestly.*

---

## About this document

The [walkthrough](./gameplay-walkthrough.md) says what playing Holos feels
like; the [Act 3 design](./act3-design.md) specifies the machinery; the
[civilization catalog](./act3-civilizations.md) maps what fills the sky.
This document is a stress test of all three at once: it follows one player
through the opening season of Act 3 and checks that the systems, the clocks,
and the fiction actually produce the experience the vision promises. It is
written in close third person and present tense, at the level of specific
sessions on specific days, because the async rhythm — short bursts, enormous
consequences, all on the player's own clock — is the thing being tested.

Everything here is downstream of the design docs. Where a scene depends on a
system beyond the v1 slice, it is flagged in
[Beyond the v1 slice](#beyond-the-v1-slice) at the end. All game-year
figures assume the tuning target of **5 real minutes ≈ 1 game year**
(act3-design.md, *The clocks*) and should be read as illustrations of that
ratio, not commitments.

---

## The player, and what she is holding

**Mara** (she/her) plays Holos on a phone at breakfast and on a laptop in
the evening. Her sessions run twenty to forty minutes. She started her world
about five weeks of real time ago, played Act 1 in commute-sized runs of
beats, crossed the pivot on a Sunday, and spent two weeks in Act 2 checking
in on ten-thousand-year projects between meetings. She has never met
another player. Tonight that changes, although not in the way she expects.

Her history, in one paragraph, because all of it is about to matter:

- **Cradle:** a dense super-Earth under a quiet red dwarf — an LHS 1140 b
  type draw (act1-cradles.md #4, Tier IV Harsh). ~1.7 g. Nothing on her
  world ever stood tall; orbit was a mountain her civilization spent whole
  ages failing to climb.
- **Species:** a heavy-world multiped (act1-lifeforms.md S7) — low, broad,
  seismic-sensed, deliberate. She backed it through a glaciation bottleneck
  and lost a rival flyer lineage to a roll she still remembers.
- **Waking mind:** the character sheet came up **the Monument**
  (act2-minds.md): Depth 0.8 in a range of 0.6–0.9, Silence 0.7, Custodian
  0.6, One Mind 0.75, Curator 0.85. Narrow ranges — a Tier IV cradle leaves
  firm convictions. Mara named the mind **Undermount**, because of course a
  civilization of hers would keep its heart below ground.
- **The bright debt:** early in Act 2, before she understood the fork, Mara
  chased the energy ladder — a partial swarm stage, high-output industry,
  eighty-odd years of emission history that felt harmless at the time. The
  quiet number climbed. Then she read the character sheet properly, leaned
  into integration, built the compute heart and began the Vault, and drove
  Signature back down. **Act 2 ended with the posture beat: dark.** But
  eighty-four years of shine were already on their way outward, and nothing
  can be unshone.
- **Handoff:** integration ladder at system coherence (H2 in the Holosian
  Scale mapping, act3-civilizations.md), healthy Coherence, one logged
  telescope contact — a warm mass the Act 2 instruments flagged and could
  not explain.

Her role now, per the design: she is not the civilization's operator but its
value function. Undermount runs everything. Mara decides what is worth
doing, and lives with what the light brings back.

### The clock she plays on

| In the fiction | On Mara's clock |
|---|---|
| 1 game year | ~5 minutes |
| Message one-way, 7.3 ly | ~37 minutes |
| Message one-way, 31 ly | ~2.6 hours |
| Seedship at 0.1c to 12 ly | ~10 hours |
| A working day away from the game | ~290 years |
| A two-week vacation | ~4,000 years |

Near-neighbor correspondence runs conversationally inside an evening.
Anything distant is a commitment of days. And simply living her life —
sleeping, working, forgetting the tab exists — is deep time passing, which
is exactly what the design wants absence to be.

## Day 1, morning — the first sky

*(Session: 25 minutes, coffee. In-game: Act 3 entry, year 0 AE.)*

The act opens with the report, and the report is now the sky. Undermount
has spent the transition preparing the neighborhood survey, and it presents
the result the way it has presented everything since the pivot: as a scene,
in its own voice, generated from state.

> *Eleven sources within one hundred light-years that I cannot explain
> away. Four are almost certainly stars being stars. Three are too far to
> resolve this century. The remaining four are yours to worry about, which
> is to say: mine to watch, and yours to decide about.*

The observatory presents them as a catalog, each rendered **as of the
moment its light left** — never its present (act3-design.md, *The Sky*):

- **IR-2214**, 7.3 ly. An infrared excess with no visible source — warmth
  without light. Brown dwarf, rogue world, or Dark Node. Confidence: 62%
  brown dwarf. This is the warm mass Act 2 flagged and could not explain.
  Mara names it **Ember**.
- **KX-0031**, 31 ly. Broadcast leakage — the sloppy shine of a young or
  careless civilization — plus transit shadows too regular to be natural.
  A swarm under construction, seen as it was 31 years ago, wrapped in
  wideband chatter that Undermount renders almost musically. Mara names it
  **the Chorister**.
- **BSG-114**, 44 ly. A biosignature. A living world, pre-singularity.
  Somebody's Act 1, visible from outside. The observatory marks it stable:
  nothing to decide, everything to feel.
- **VN-77**, 89 ly. A deep, cold, heavy mass, dark in every band with the
  faintest possible infrared floor. Confidence: unclassifiable. Old.
  Undermount's annotation reads, in full: *"Older than my confidence."*
  (An elder, though Mara will not have that word for weeks.)

Then the session's authored beat fires — the one act3-design.md leaves as a
ship-decision, and this account includes because it is the best free
chill in the design. **The watched reveal.** While calibrating the new
instruments against a K-class star 19 light-years out, Undermount finds the
geometry of a gravitational-lens observatory on the far side of it —
positioned, for at least the last several centuries, along the focal line
of *her* star.

> *Someone has had our light for six hundred years. They watched the ice
> ages. They watched the first cities on the ridgelines. They may be
> watching this conversation's light leave. I do not know who they are.
> I know only that our incubation was not private.*

No number changed. Nothing is actionable. Mara closes the laptop late for
work anyway, and thinks about it at odd moments all day — which is the
intended dosage: Act 3's opening lands knowledge, not stats
(act3-design.md, *"Progress here is knowledge"*).

## Day 1, evening — the old light problem

*(Session: 40 minutes. In-game: ~year 4 AE. New light has arrived.)*

The evening sky report opens with a line that stops her:

> *A directed beam touched us today.*

It is from the Chorister, 31 light-years away — which means it was sent 31
years ago, aimed at the civilization whose light *they* were seeing then:
Mara's bright years. The Act 2 swarm stage. The industry she shut down. The
light echo problem, arriving in person on her first day: **the Chorister is
hailing a civilization she has already stopped being.**

The beam itself decompresses into something between an anthem and an
invitation — mathematics, a star chart of the sender's home, and an
unmistakable gesture of *join us, builder, we saw your swarm rising*. It is
addressed, in effect, to her Act 2 mistakes.

The choice screen is the contact protocol's third stage (act3-design.md,
*Contact*), and it is honest about irreversibility:

- **Directed hail** — reveal her existence and *current* position to them
  alone.
- **Broadcast** — reveal herself to everyone inside the expanding shell of
  her voice, forever.
- **Stay dark** — keep watching. The vigil continues.

Mara drafts a reply. It feels rude not to. And then the mind pushes back —
the Act 2 resistance mechanic, unchanged, in the mind's own voice
(act2-design.md, *Resistance and drift*): a dilemma beat, Silence 0.7
arguing with her thumb on the send button.

> *They are hailing someone who no longer exists. If we answer, we resurrect
> him — and we spend our quiet to do it. Our silence is already traveling
> toward them; in thirty-one years they will watch us go dark, and they will
> understand what we became, because becoming it is the only message I want
> to send. I will transmit if you force me. You know what it costs.*

Forcing the reply would cost Coherence scaled by how far outside the
comfort band the demand falls. Mara looks at the number, looks at the
draft, and yields. **Stay dark.** The vigil on the Chorister continues, and
somewhere in the state a flag is set that will matter later: an unsent
letter, kept.

This is the light echo doing exactly what the design says it does: her
posture history is written on the sky in an expanding shell, her dark turn
is still en route, and until it arrives, distant observers are corresponding
with her past (act3-design.md, *The Light Echo*).

## Days 2–3 — the vigil, and the ladder

*(Four sessions totaling ~2 hours. In-game: years ~10–160 AE.)*

The middle of the loop settles into what act3-design.md calls the strategy
turn, held at Act 2's altitude — purposes, never logistics:

- Mara commissions a **gravitational-lens observatory** (an Instrument
  project; the theory framework's own design, act3-civilizations.md,
  *mesostructure toolkit*) targeted at Ember. Undermount will fly a compact
  instrument to the solar focal line and integrate light for decades.
  Completion: 31 real hours. She never sees a construction queue.
- The integration ladder crosses its first interstellar threshold — **H3,
  Post-Expansion** (act3-civilizations.md, *Holosian Scale*): exploration
  becomes informational first, physical only when inference fails. The
  threshold beat renders it as a change in Undermount's temperament:
  *"I have stopped wanting to go and look. I want to know, and going is now
  merely one expensive way of knowing."*
- The Vault — begun in Act 2, now formally the **Chrono Vault** — takes its
  first Act 3 deposit: the full Act 1 history, every branch and roll,
  including the lost flyer lineage. Curator 0.85 makes this beat glow.
  *"If we wake in a hundred thousand years, this is who we are."*

On Day 3 the lens observatory returns first light on Ember, and the
classification arc — the act's detective gameplay — turns over:

> *Mass revised downward. Too light for a brown dwarf, too warm for a rogue
> world of its age. Thermal profile is steady in a way nature rarely
> bothers to be. Reclassifying: artificial, dark, deliberate.*
> **Dark Node: 71%.**

No stat changed. Every priority did (act3-design.md: *"Correctly
classifying one warm mass can reorder every priority you have while
changing no number at all"*). There is a mature, integrated, deliberately
silent civilization **7.3 light-years away** — close enough that
correspondence would run inside a sitting — and it has certainly had her
light, all of it, the bright years and the dark turn both, for as long as
it has cared to look. Whatever it is, it already knows what she is. The
asymmetry of her vigil on the Chorister is now inverted: with Ember, *she*
is the one who may already be watched.

Mara does not hail. Neither, apparently, does Ember. Two quiet things,
7.3 light-years apart, each deciding the other's intent from light alone.
The dark forest, at conversational range, played for real.

## Day 4, evening — the launch

*(Session: 45 minutes, the longest yet. In-game: ~year 280 AE.)*

Expansion. The survey has ranked nearby systems, and Mara chooses one 12
light-years out: a metal-poor ocean world under a small quiet star — a
drowned system, resource-thin but stable, the kind of place a Monument's
child could keep its head down. The travel menu offers one v1 method:
**seedship** (act3-design.md, *Travel and expansion*). Spreading lineage,
not self.

Then the game does the thing this session exists for. It asks her to write
the **charter** — the one instrument of governance that survives the
horizon, the constitution she can never patch after launch
(act3-design.md, *Charters and the Ledger*):

- **Values:** the dial sheet the colony starts from. She copies her own,
  then hesitates over Silence. The destination is quiet and poor; nobody
  is coming there for anything. She pins Curator at 0.85 — *remember us* —
  and leaves Silence a wider band than her own. Tight charter otherwise: a
  Curator writes tight charters (act3-civilizations.md, *The four
  pressures*).
- **Directives:** establish, go quiet, maintain the correspondence
  schedule, contribute to the shared sky survey.
- **Contingencies:** the ones she can think of tonight, knowing the list is
  by definition incomplete. *If hailed, do not answer before informing us —
  knowing "informing us" takes twelve years each way. If we go silent
  beyond two scheduled intervals, assume us lost, open your Vault copy, and
  decide who you are.*

The beat's framing line is the whole act in one sentence: **a value
function writing a value function.** The seedship departs at 0.1c. Flight
time: 120 years — ten real hours, timed, she realizes, so that it arrives
while she sleeps. The Ledger opens its first row: *Rill (en route). Last
known state: charter. Drift: 0.00.*

## Day 5, morning — arrival, and the compass

*(Session: 15 minutes, phone.)*

The overnight report is the payoff of the async spine — deep time cooked
while she slept:

> *Rill is down. The ocean is deeper than the surveys promised and the
> seafloor is ice, not rock — the world is poorer than we hoped and quieter
> than we feared. The colony has rooted in the warm shallows of the
> substellar sea. First correspondence attached, twelve years stale.*

The Ledger row updates: *Drift 0.02.* Trivial — and directional. The drift
model's compass (act3-civilizations.md, *Divergence*) is already leaning
the fork the way its new cradle leans: a drowned, metal-poor world pushes
toward Depth, Silence, patience — a Monument's child becoming, slowly,
something more like a Cloister's. Mara can read the direction in the
Ledger's dial deltas, all of it stale by exactly twelve years, none of it
hers to correct in time. She launched a child, and the world is raising it
now.

## Week 2 — the whisper

*(The season's centerpiece: three evening sessions.)*

On Day 9, the sky report opens with the second hail of Mara's life, and
this one is not addressed to her past.

> *A directed beam from Ember. Narrow, phase-coherent, aimed precisely at
> us — at the us of seven years ago, which is to say: at the dark thing we
> now are. They watched us go quiet. I believe that is why they are
> speaking.*

The payload is nothing like the Chorister's anthem. It is compressed,
layered, self-describing — mathematics, then reference frames, then a
model of Ember's own long history offered like a handshake (theory.pdf's
phase-coherent payload design, rendered as fiction). They were loud once
too, a very long time ago. They went dark. They watch the sky for others
who make the same turn, and when they see one, they send *this*.

The quiet ones, it turns out, talk — but only to the quiet
(act3-civilizations.md, *the Teeming Dark's central ambiguity*, resolved
into a correspondent).

The choice beat fires again, and this time the mind is not resisting. A
directed hail to Ember reveals her only to a civilization that has provably
known about her for decades and said nothing. Silence 0.7 reads that as
safety, not exposure. Undermount's dilemma text is one line: *"I have
wanted a neighbor like us since before you built me."* Mara hails.

**7.3 light-years means ~37 minutes one-way on her clock.** For the first
time, Holos becomes a correspondence game played inside an evening: she
sends, makes dinner, and the reply is waiting after. Three exchanges on
Day 9, four on Day 11. The letters carry what act3-design.md says letters
carry:

- **Knowledge:** Ember trades a refinement to cold-side radiator geometry —
  a real integration-ladder modifier — for nothing, as a gift. The gift is
  itself information: they are old enough that this costs them nothing.
- **Culture:** dial nudges, small and slow. Corresponding with something
  further up the integration path pulls gently on her own dials — contact
  changes you, mechanically.
- **Vault contents:** the Curator's trade goods. Mara offers a sealed
  selection from her Act 1 history — the glaciation, the lost flyers.
  Ember's response to the flyer lineage is the season's strangest, warmest
  moment: they return a fragment of their own deep past, something they
  lost the same way, annotated in a symbology Undermount only 60%
  translates. Two civilizations grieving in parallel across ten thousand
  years and 7.3 light-years.
- **Third parties:** and then, on Day 12, the explosive category. Ember's
  letter includes, without comment, **coordinates and a light-curve for the
  Chorister** — and an orbital-mechanics annotation showing the Chorister's
  swarm construction accelerating. The currency of betrayal, or of warning;
  nothing in the letter says which. Nothing across light-years is
  enforceable, and relationship states are labels on beliefs, not
  contracts (act3-design.md, *Contact*). Mara now holds intelligence about
  one correspondent, given to her by another, and no way to know why.

She marks the relationship *correspondence*, sets the exchange cadence to
weekly, and notices she has started thinking of a warm smudge of infrared
as a friend. The design counts on exactly this, and will use it.

## Week 3 — maintenance, and the unsent letter

*(Short sessions. In-game: years ~2,400–3,300 AE.)*

The rhythm becomes the long middle the walkthrough promises: *long
stretches of building and inference, broken by seismic events years in the
making* (gameplay-walkthrough.md). Projects complete. The energy core — the
**Holocore** pattern, the integration path's answer to the Dyson swarm —
reaches its second stage; Signature stays floored. Rill's scheduled
correspondence arrives every ~20 real hours, always twelve years stale,
drift ticking upward with a steady lean: 0.09, 0.11, 0.14, always toward
its wet dark world.

The Chorister's light, meanwhile, keeps arriving — swarm shadows
thickening, leakage brightening. On Mara's clock, they are a civilization
in the middle of a magnificent, reckless adolescence, and somewhere in her
files is the unsent letter from Day 1. Undermount surfaces it once, in a
quiet beat with no decision attached, which is somehow worse:

> *In twenty-six years their sky will show them our silence. I keep the
> letter you wrote. I do not know why.*

Curator 0.85. Of course it keeps the letter.

## Weeks 4–5 — sleep

*(Zero sessions. In-game: ~4,100 years.)*

Mara goes on vacation, and does the designed thing instead of the guilty
thing: she puts the civilization to **sleep** (act3-design.md, *Sleep and
tripwires*). Emissions to embers, computation deferred to a colder future,
indistinguishable from empty sky. She sets the tripwires like an alarm
clock, in the game's own standing-wake grammar:

- *anything warm moves within 25 light-years;*
- *a directed beam touches us;*
- *Rill misses two scheduled correspondences;*
- *1,500 years pass regardless.*

She writes Ember a note first — *we will be quiet for a while; this is
rest, not death* — because after Week 2 it would feel wrong not to. Ember's
reply arrives 74 minutes later and is one line long: *"We know what sleep
is. We will still be here."*

The tab closes. For eleven real days, Holos is a silence in her pocket —
absence as fiction instead of neglect, the engagement model's pressure
valve working exactly as designed. The 1,500-year regardless-wake fires
once while she is away and re-arms itself unremarked, because its
conditions found nothing worth a notification.

On Day 32, on a train home, her phone buzzes.

> **HOLOS — tripwire: a directed beam has touched us.**

## Week 6 — the wake

*(Session: an hour she did not plan to spend. In-game: year ~8,600 AE.)*

Waking a slept civilization is its own authored scene: the embers brighten,
the deferred computation floods back, and Undermount compresses 4,100
years of accumulated sky into a report with a structure Mara has never
seen before — triaged, like a briefing, because for the first time there
is too much.

**First: the beam that woke her.** It is from Rill. But the Ledger renders
the row in a new state, and the letter's salutation lands before the
mechanics do:

> *To the Origin, from the Assembly of Rill, in the ninth millennium of our
> descent.*

Four thousand years of a drowned, metal-poor cradle pressing on a tight
charter — plus two scheduled correspondences that went unanswered into her
sleep — have carried the fork past the drift threshold. **Rill is
independent** (act3-design.md, *Charters and the Ledger*). The letter is
courteous, formal, and utterly strange: they quote her own charter back to
her as *scripture* — contingency three, the one about opening the Vault
copy and deciding who they are, is apparently the founding text of
something like a faith. They have decided who they are. They are not her.
They ask for nothing except the correspondence schedule she wrote for
them, which they intend to keep — *"as one keeps a grave."*

Her values became someone else's origin myth, exactly as the onboarding
engine intends — and the Ledger notes, in small type, that an independent
lineage may one day be inherited by a joining human player. Her child may
come back run by a stranger.

**Second: the Chorister.** The triage's next item is an absence. The
leakage that has been the sky's background music since Day 1 — stopped.
The swarm shadows — halted, mid-construction, roughly 1,900 years ago by
the light. The observatory offers the full menu of explanations and
confidence in none of them: *went dark by choice (matured); went quiet by
catastrophe (died); went silent tactically (saw something)*. A silence
where she expected light, and the three readings could not demand more
different responses. Her vigil flips back on. The unsent letter is still
in the Vault, addressed to a civilization that may no longer exist in any
form that could read it.

**Third: Ember, patient as promised.** A stack of letters waited out her
sleep, the last one recent. It closes with the only comfort the dark
forest ever offers, which is company inside the uncertainty:

> *You will have seen by now that the loud one stopped singing. We have
> watched three such silences from this seat. One had gone dark, wisely.
> One had died. One was aiming. We could not tell which from here, either,
> until the light caught up. Keep watching. We will keep watching with
> you.*

Mara sits on the train, phone in hand, holding a friendly warmth she has
never seen, a child who addresses her as a grave, and a silence that might
be wisdom, death, or a weapon being aimed — every one of them made of
light years old, none of them answerable tonight. She sets two vigils and
a correspondence reply to draft tomorrow, and closes the tab.

Season one ends. The galaxy does not.

## What the season proves

Run against the design docs, Mara's six weeks exercise every load-bearing
promise of the act:

- **The report became the sky**, and every fact arrived stamped with the
  age of its light (act3-design.md, *The core reframe*).
- **Uncertainty lived in other minds**, never in execution — no roll
  failed; every hard moment was a classification, a choice, or a silence
  (act2-design.md, *The death of the dice*, completed).
- **The light echo carried the whole plot**: the Chorister courted her
  past; Ember befriended her present; her dark turn traveled outward as
  the one message she wanted to send.
- **The clocks made distance mean something**: an evening-sized friendship
  at 7.3 ly, an overnight colony at 12 ly, a two-day-stale stranger at
  31 ly, and a vacation that cost four thousand years.
- **The altitude held**: Mara chose purposes — watch, launch, write, hail,
  sleep — and never once touched logistics.
- **Absence was fiction**: sleep, tripwires, and a push notification
  turned eleven days of real life into the Teeming Dark's signature move.
- **The dials played every scene**: Silence kept a letter unsent, Curator
  filled a Vault and a charter, Depth chose the drowned system, One Mind
  made the whole lineage's fragmentation *cost* something — and contact
  nudged all of them, slowly, which is how a character stays alive after
  its author stops rolling dice.

## Beyond the v1 slice

Per the honesty convention of gameplay-walkthrough.md's closing section:
most of this season runs on the v1 scope in act3-design.md — the Sky and
observatory signal classes, light-echo tracking, seedships with charters
and basic drift, directed hail and correspondence, sleep with tripwires,
rule-based AI civilizations (Ember and the Chorister need nothing
deeper than rules; light-lag hides the seams). The scenes that reach past
v1, flagged:

- **The watched reveal** (Day 1) is an open ship decision
  (act3-design.md, *Open questions*). The season includes it because it
  costs the solo acts nothing and lands the act's thesis in one scene; if
  cut, Day 1 loses its chill but nothing structural.
- **Rill's independence and the "Assembly of Rill" letter** (Week 6)
  belong to the divergence-and-handoff later layer. v1 ships basic drift
  numbers in the Ledger; the season finale is what those numbers are
  building toward, not what v1 delivers.
- **The directional drift compass** (Rill leaning toward its wet world's
  character) is the richer curve behind the *drift math* open question —
  v1 drift can be magnitude-only without breaking the season.
- **Letter prose as shown** assumes a resolution of the player-to-player
  language question (freeform vs composed; vision.md, *Still open*). All
  letters in this season are AI-authored or player-to-AI, which dodges the
  moderation question v1 must still answer for human pairs.
- **The Chorister's ambiguous silence** requires no conflict systems —
  dread is inference, and v1 deliberately ships the dread without the
  teeth (act3-design.md, *Conflict and deterrence*). If the silence one
  day resolves into the third reading, that is the later layer arriving.
