# Holos — Player Conduct

Holos is a game about deception, silence, and betrayal between
civilizations. All of that is play, and none of it is moderated. This
page is about the one line the game does draw — between what a
civilization does to a civilization, and what a person does to a person.

## The line

**Betrayal is play; abuse is not.** Lying in a letter, performing a
false character, leaking a rival's coordinates, striking first — that is
the game, and no report about it will be actioned. Directing hostility
at the *human being* behind a civilization is not the game, and is not
tolerated.

## Not tolerated

- Harassment, slurs, hate speech, or threats, in any player-authored
  text (letters, names, or any surface added later).
- Personal attacks on another player — the person, not their
  civilization.
- Doxxing or attempting to identify the human behind a civilization.
- Sexual content directed at another player.
- Stalking or pursuing a specific player across cohorts, sessions, or
  accounts to make the game unplayable for them. (In-fiction rivalry
  within a galaxy, however ruthless, is play.)
- Cheating: exploiting bugs for advantage, botting, or tampering with
  the client or protocol. Found an exploit? Report it instead.
- Spam.

## Tools and enforcement

- **Report.** Any letter or player-authored text can be reported;
  reports are reviewed by a human. (Cohorts are small; review is
  reading.)
- **Block.** When correspondence ships, any player can go dark to a
  correspondent — a blocked civilization's letters simply never arrive
  again. No notification, no drama.
- **Enforcement** is proportionate and boring: a warning, then removal
  from the cohort, then the account. In-game consequences are never
  used as punishment and punishments are never dressed up as fiction.

## One standing rule for the design

Every new surface that carries player-authored text ships with the
answer to "how is this reported, and can it reach someone who blocked
its author?" — decided at design time, not after the first incident.
