# Intelligent life — the species catalog

*The space of biological intelligences that can climb out of the cradles.
This is the Act 1 tier: the technological, tool-using species a world raises
before the singularity. It is the bridge between [act1-cradles.md](./act1-cradles.md)
(the worlds) and the post-singularity minds of Acts 2–3.*

> Related design: [act1-cradles.md](./act1-cradles.md) (the 40 worlds these species
> evolve on), [vision.md](./vision.md) (§ Act 1 — the cradle's fingerprint),
> [gameplay-walkthrough.md](./gameplay-walkthrough.md) (§ Act 1 — the climb),
> [act2-design.md](./act2-design.md) (the dials these species seed).

---

## What this is

The cradle sets the pressures; life is what answers them. This catalog maps
the **intelligent, technology-capable species** those pressures can produce —
not every organism a biosphere grows, but the lineages that can reach the top
of Act 1's climb: *first life → complex life → tool use → culture → a
technological civilization at the threshold of superintelligence.*

Three things to hold onto:

- **The cradle constrains, it does not dictate.** A single world can breed
  several candidate lineages. Which one crosses the threshold is exactly what
  Act 1's branching history decides — and the lineages that *don't* make it are
  the raw material of its bottleneck beats (the ones you shelter, back, or let
  fall). See *How this feeds Act 1*, below.
- **Intelligence is not the hard part; technology is.** Big brains are cheap
  compared to the road from a clever animal to a spacefaring civilization. That
  road needs four things at once (the *technogenesis filter*, below), and some
  cradles make one or more of them nearly impossible. That difficulty is the
  same difficulty act1-cradles.md already prices in.
- **The body writes the character.** A distributed octopus-mind, a hive, a
  song-culture of tool-less swimmers, and a heavy-world tool-user do not merely
  *look* different — they arrive at the singularity *wanting* different things.
  Each entry ends with a **dial seed**, consistent with the cradle logic in
  act1-cradles.md, that carries into the Act 2 character sheet.

## The technogenesis filter

To climb from animal to technological civilization, a lineage needs **all four**
of these. Missing or crippling any one is a great filter, and it is where the
harshest cradles bite.

1. **Manipulation.** Something that can precisely handle the world — hands,
   tentacles, claws, trunks, manipulator castes. No manipulator, no tools, no
   technology. (Song-cultures and networked minds can be brilliant and still be
   stuck here.)
2. **Energy mastery.** A path to controlled, concentrated energy — the
   fire-equivalent. On land this is fire. Underwater it is nearly barred, and a
   lineage must find another door: hydrothermal-vent metallurgy, bioelectric
   discharge, or engineered biochemistry. Worlds where this door is shut are the
   Brutal ones.
3. **Symbolic communication.** A channel rich enough to carry culture across
   generations — sound, light, chemistry, electricity, vibration — and abstract
   enough to encode ideas, not just signals.
4. **External memory.** A way to store knowledge outside a single body:
   writing, carving, chemical marking, inherited structures, a shared network.
   Culture that can only live in living memory has a ceiling.

**Technogenesis difficulty** is rated on each species below:
**Straightforward · Hard · Severe · Near-impossible.** It tracks — but is not
identical to — the cradle's difficulty tier: a gentle world can still raise a
lineage with a hard body plan, and a brutal world can, rarely, raise a
straightforward one.

## The axes of variation

Every intelligent species is a point in this space. The named archetypes below
are the populated, technology-plausible regions of it — not the whole space,
which is effectively infinite.

- **Medium** — aquatic · terrestrial · aerial/buoyant · subsurface/sealed ·
  cryogenic. Sets almost everything downstream, especially the energy door.
- **Body plan** — bilateral · radial · colonial/modular · networked/substrate ·
  composite (symbiotic). Sets manipulation and the shape of cognition.
- **Energy base** — photosynthesis-derived · chemosynthesis (vents) ·
  geothermal · predation. Sets metabolic tempo and where life concentrates.
- **Sensory suite** — vision (and at what wavelengths) · echolocation/sonar ·
  electroreception · chemoreception · thermal/IR · seismic/tactile. Dim red
  suns, dark oceans, and haze worlds each favor different primary senses.
- **Signal channel** — acoustic · bioluminescent/visual · chemical · bioelectric
  · vibrational. This becomes the civilization's first medium and shadows how it
  will one day want to be heard (or not) across the galaxy.
- **Cognition model** — unitary (one brain, one self) · distributed (one body,
  many semi-autonomous centers) · collective (many bodies, one emergent mind) ·
  networked (a substrate that thinks slowly and everywhere) · composite (a mind
  that is a partnership). This is the deepest fork, and it pre-writes the
  **One Mind ↔ Chorus** dial before the singularity is even in sight.
- **Sociality** — solitary · pair/pod · herd/migratory · eusocial · sessile
  colony.

## The catalog

Twenty archetypes, grouped by cognition model and medium. Each is a region of
the space above, chosen because a plausible road to technology runs through it.

---

### A. Oceanic and aquatic minds

*The galaxy's most common habitable environments are wet, so most candidate
lineages are aquatic — and most of them run straight into the energy door.
Ocean worlds are common in the pool and Brutal to climb for exactly this reason.*

#### S1. Tentacled cephalopodan
- **Origin cradles:** ocean & eyeball worlds — TRAPPIST-1f (#2), TOI-700 d (#6), eyeball ice world (#14), Kepler-22b (#24), Kepler-62f (#18), and the drowned worlds (#16, #12).
- **Medium & body plan:** aquatic, soft-bodied, radial-to-bilateral; many prehensile arms; jet and undulation locomotion.
- **Senses & signal:** superb vision; **chromatophore** signalling — talking in skin-color and pattern, a bandwidth-rich, instantly-readable, utterly public language.
- **Sociality & cognition:** solitary-to-loose, **distributed cognition** — much of the mind lives in the arms. The self is already a federation before it is a civilization.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** superb manipulation, the best in the water — but underwater, energy mastery is the wall. A cephalopodan reaches technology only through vent-smithing or bioelectric/biochemical routes. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Chorus** (a distributed body raises a distributed mind), lean **Depth**, curious-neutral Custodian.

#### S2. Song-culture pelagic
- **Origin cradles:** open ocean worlds — TOI-700 d (#6), Kepler-22b (#24), Kepler-62f (#18), the metal-poor drowned world (#16), high-obliquity migratory seas (#38).
- **Medium & body plan:** aquatic, large, streamlined, **fin-limbed** — built to swim, not to handle.
- **Senses & signal:** **sonar and song** — a rich acoustic culture that carries oral history across ocean basins.
- **Sociality & cognition:** deeply social pods; large brains; unitary selves bound by culture.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** the tragic case. Enormous cognition, real culture, and **no hands** — fins and mouths cannot build. Reaches technology only by co-opting a manipulator (see S14) or through slow biotech; more often it is a magnificent dead end that Act 1 must route around. **Technogenesis: Near-impossible (alone).**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Voice** (a species that is its songs), **Custodian** (empathic, social), lean Chorus.

#### S3. Clawed benthic exoskeletal
- **Origin cradles:** shallow/floored ocean worlds — LHS 1140 b (#4), TOI-700 d (#6), Kepler-62f (#18), tidally-heated moon (#15), crushing ocean super-Earths (#40).
- **Medium & body plan:** aquatic-to-amphibious, segmented exoskeleton, **claws and manipulating limbs**, benthic crawler that can haul out onto land or vent fields.
- **Senses & signal:** vision plus chemoreception and vibration through the substrate; signalling by gesture, sound-taps, and chemistry.
- **Sociality & cognition:** variable — solitary hunters to loose aggregations; unitary cognition.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** the **best aquatic tech path**. Claws build; an amphibious benthic can reach shorelines and vents where the energy door cracks open — the likeliest lineage to actually carry an ocean world to technology. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** lean **Instrumental** (a builder-predator), neutral One Mind / Chorus, lean Reach if it makes landfall.

#### S4. Electro-sensing abyssal
- **Origin cradles:** lightless and sealed waters — subsurface-ocean ice world (#36), TRAPPIST-1f (#2), tidally-heated moon (#15), rogue geothermal world (#39), hycean K2-18 b (#12).
- **Medium & body plan:** aquatic, deep or ice-sealed, bilateral; built for pressure and dark.
- **Senses & signal:** **electroreception and chemoreception** primary; little or no vision. Communicates in electric fields and chemical plumes.
- **Sociality & cognition:** solitary-to-small-group; unitary, patient cognition tuned to a world with no horizon and no sky.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulators adequate; energy from vents is *right there*, which is the one advantage of the abyss — but the lineage has no concept of "outside," no sky to aim at, and must discover the universe before it can want to reach it. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Silence** (unseeing, unseen), **Depth**, lean Instrumental.

#### S5. Colonial reef superorganism
- **Origin cradles:** stable warm shallow seas — Kepler-22b (#24), TOI-700 d (#6), temperate twin (#22), Kepler-62f (#18).
- **Medium & body plan:** aquatic, **sessile colonial** — a reef that is one distributed organism of specialized polyps.
- **Senses & signal:** chemical and electrical signalling across the colony; diffuse light and current sensing.
- **Sociality & cognition:** the colony *is* the individual; cognition is emergent, slow, and everywhere at once.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** almost no manipulation and no mobility — it cannot reach for tools, so it must grow its technology, shaping living structures and chemistry rather than building. A profoundly alien, uphill road. **Technogenesis: Near-impossible.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **One Mind** (a single body of many parts), **Depth**, lean **Custodian**.

### B. Terrestrial bilateral tool-users

*Dry land opens the energy door — fire is possible — so the most
straightforward roads to technology run here. This is the convergent
"default," and the game should make it feel earned, not given.*

#### S6. Erect generalist
- **Origin cradles:** temperate land worlds — the temperate twin (#22), Kepler-442b (#17), Ross 128 b (#5), 40 Eridani A b (#20), Gliese 12 b (#10), bright super-Earth (#29), Kepler-452b (#23).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, bilateral, upright or semi-upright, **freed forelimbs with fine manipulators**, forward-facing senses.
- **Senses & signal:** vision-dominant (tuned to the host star — red-shifted under an M dwarf); **vocal language**; hands that also gesture.
- **Sociality & cognition:** social bands; unitary self; culture in stories, then writing.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** all four filters open at once — the classic, most *tractable* climb. Fire, tools, speech, writing. **Technogenesis: Straightforward.**
- **Dial seed:** near-balanced, the widest post-cradle latitude of any archetype; a faint **Voice**. What Act 1 chooses matters most here.

#### S7. Heavy-world multiped
- **Origin cradles:** high-gravity worlds — crushing super-Earth (#40), LHS 1140 b (#4), HD 40307 g (#19), Luyten b (#11), Kepler-442b (#17), super-Mercury (#33), Kapteyn b (#30), Tau Ceti f (#25).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, **low, broad, and strong**; many legs for stability under crushing gravity; one or two limb-pairs specialized into manipulators.
- **Senses & signal:** vision plus strong **seismic/tactile** sense (heavy ground carries vibration well); low, powerful vocalizations.
- **Sociality & cognition:** cohesive groups; unitary, deliberate cognition; a culture built close to the ground.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation and fire both available — but the gravity well is a lifelong ceiling. It masters its surface early and finds *leaving* the hardest thing it will ever do. (On iron/metal-rich worlds like #33, metallurgy is native and easy — a compensating gift.) **Technogenesis: Straightforward on the surface; Hard to reach orbit.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Depth** (the well fights every ambition outward), lean **One Mind**, lean **Custodian** on rich worlds.

#### S8. Twilight-band migratory
- **Origin cradles:** terminator worlds — TRAPPIST-1e (#1), Proxima b (#3), Ross 128 b (#5), Gliese 12 b (#10), terminator storm world (#13).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, bilateral, built for the **narrow habitable ring** between permanent day and night; often migratory along the band.
- **Senses & signal:** **thermal/IR vision** across the huge day-night gradient plus red-tuned sight under a dim sun; vocal and gestural signalling.
- **Sociality & cognition:** mobile bands strung along the terminator; unitary selves; a culture organized around the fixed sun and the trek.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** hands and fire both available; the challenge is a *linear, crowded* world with little room and a savage climate at each edge — technology comes, but under permanent scarcity of space. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** lean **One Mind** (a single connected ring), **Voice** (a sky owned by one unmissable sun and close sister worlds), lean Instrumental under storm/flare pressure.

### C. Aerial and buoyant minds

*Thick atmospheres and low gravity make flight and even permanent buoyancy
cheap. Wide senses, wide range — but flight and heavy tools are in tension.*

#### S9. Winged grasping flyer
- **Origin cradles:** thick-air / low-to-moderate-gravity worlds — GJ 667 Cc (#8), circumbinary world (#32), bright super-Earth (#29), 40 Eridani A b (#20), temperate twin (#22).
- **Medium & body plan:** aerial-terrestrial, winged, with **grasping feet or a freed limb-pair**; lands to build.
- **Senses & signal:** acute long-range vision; rich vocal/song signalling carried on the air.
- **Sociality & cognition:** flocks; unitary selves; a wide-horizon culture, early astronomers by instinct.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation is real but competes with flight for the same limbs and against the weight of tools; fire is available on the ground. A wide-ranging, outward-looking climber. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** lean **Reach** (mastery of the open air, a taste for range), **Voice** (a sky-facing, far-seeing species), neutral elsewhere.

#### S10. Buoyant aerial drifter
- **Origin cradles:** thick/deep atmospheres — hycean K2-18 b (#12), Titan-analog haze world (#37), gas-enveloped worlds.
- **Medium & body plan:** **permanently airborne**, gasbag or lift-body; radial-to-modular; slow.
- **Senses & signal:** pressure, chemical, and acoustic sensing through a hazed or opaque sky; long, slow calls.
- **Sociality & cognition:** dispersed drifting aggregations; distributed or loosely collective cognition.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** the wall is total — nothing solid to stand on, nothing concentrated to burn, weak manipulation. Technology, if it comes, is chemical and biological, grown in the air. **Technogenesis: Near-impossible.**
- **Dial seed:** **Depth** and **Silence** (a world with no ground and no clear sky), lean Chorus.

### D. Colonial and distributed cognition

*Minds that are not one animal. These pre-write the One Mind ↔ Chorus dial hard,
and they can route around the manipulation filter through numbers and
specialization even when no single body could.*

#### S11. Eusocial hive superorganism
- **Origin cradles:** patchy and scarce worlds — cold-edge desert (#21), Kepler-442b (#17), HD 40307 g (#19), Tau Ceti f (#25), crushing super-Earth (#40), bright super-Earth (#29), Kepler-452b (#23).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, **eusocial colony** of specialized castes; the individual is a cell, the colony is the organism.
- **Senses & signal:** **chemical (pheromone)** signalling primary, plus touch and vibration; the colony computes with gradients.
- **Sociality & cognition:** **collective cognition** — no single mind, an emergent one; manipulator castes give it hands without any one body needing them.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** collective construction is its native genius (mounds, farms, climate control); fire and metallurgy come through worker castes. A powerful, alien, *achievable* climb. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **One Mind** (many bodies, one purpose) *and* **Instrumental** (the individual is expendable) — a combination no unitary species starts from.

#### S12. Networked substrate mind
- **Origin cradles:** ancient, stable, slow worlds — Kapteyn b (#30), Teegarden's Star b (#7), Barnard's Star b (#31 outer), old temperate worlds.
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, a **root/mycelial network** threading a landscape; no discrete individual at all.
- **Senses & signal:** chemical and electrical signalling along the network; slow, planet-scale integration.
- **Sociality & cognition:** **networked cognition** — thinks slowly and everywhere; memory is structural and effectively permanent.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** almost no manipulation and glacial tempo; its "technology" is grown and chemical, and its climb, if it happens, takes deep time — which its ancient world happens to have. **Technogenesis: Near-impossible (but patient).**
- **Dial seed:** strong **One Mind** (literally one network) and **Curator** (deep, unbroken memory), **Depth**.

#### S13. Aggregative modular organism
- **Origin cradles:** unstable, cyclic worlds — high-obliquity world (#38), circumbinary world (#32), worlds of boom-and-bust.
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial-or-aquatic units that **aggregate into a larger body and disperse again** (slime-mold / siphonophore logic).
- **Senses & signal:** chemical and tactile; the body reconfigures its senses by aggregating differently.
- **Sociality & cognition:** cognition that **assembles on demand** — solitary units, transient group minds when they mass.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation depends on configuration; energy mastery is hard; but adaptability to catastrophe is unmatched. A strange, resilient, uneven climber. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Chorus** (a self that splits and reforms), **Shedder** (it sheds units and starts over as a way of life).

#### S14. Obligate symbiotic composite
- **Origin cradles:** rich, diverse biospheres — temperate twin (#22), Kepler-442b (#17), ocean worlds where a manipulator and a cognition species meet (#24, #6).
- **Medium & body plan:** **two species, one mind** — e.g. a tool-less cognition lineage (see S2) obligately paired with a manipulator lineage (see S3); neither is intelligent alone.
- **Senses & signal:** the union's senses are the sum of both partners'; signalling bridges two channels (say, song and gesture).
- **Sociality & cognition:** **composite cognition** — a partnership that thinks; identity is inherently plural and negotiated.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** the symbiosis is precisely how a song-culture *gets hands* — the composite passes filters neither partner could alone. Rare, but a real answer to the tool-less dead end. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Chorus** *and* **Custodian** (a species whose founding lesson is that another mind is indispensable) — a distinctive, contact-friendly start.

### E. Dark and subsurface minds

*Life sheltered from a hostile sky or sealed under ice or rock. Blind, patient,
and inward — and the last to learn the universe is even there.*

#### S15. Blind seismic-thermal burrower
- **Origin cradles:** hostile-surface and cold worlds — Proxima b (#3, flare shelter), Kepler-186f (#9), terminator storm world (#13), Barnard's Star b (#31), coreless world (#35), runaway-greenhouse-edge (#27), Tau Ceti f (#25).
- **Medium & body plan:** subsurface, bilateral burrower; reduced or absent eyes; robust digging limbs that double as manipulators.
- **Senses & signal:** **seismic and thermal** sensing; touch; signalling by drumming and chemistry through the ground.
- **Sociality & cognition:** tunnel-network societies; unitary cognition tuned to a world without a horizon.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation and geothermal energy both available underground; the wall is conceptual — a species that must *dig up into the sky* before it can imagine leaving. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Silence** and **Depth** (a life spent hidden and below), neutral elsewhere.

#### S16. Chemo-lithic vent grazer
- **Origin cradles:** vent-rich and starless worlds — subsurface-ocean ice (#36), tidally-heated moon (#15), rogue geothermal world (#39), Kepler-186f (#9), Barnard's Star b (#31), carbon (#34) and lava (#26) worlds.
- **Medium & body plan:** clustered around **hydrothermal vents**; often sessile-to-slow; chemosynthetic, indifferent to sunlight.
- **Senses & signal:** chemical and thermal gradients are the whole sensory world; chemical signalling.
- **Sociality & cognition:** vent-colony societies; slow, place-bound cognition.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** energy and minerals are abundant at the vents (a rare underwater gift), but mobility and abstraction are limited; the climb is chemical and local. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** **Depth**, **Silence**, lean **Instrumental** (a life of consuming the vent).

### F. Exotic-chemistry minds

*The far edge of the possible: life on solvents other than water, or in heat and
radiation that would sterilize everything above. Rare cradles, alien outcomes,
and the game's most speculative branches.*

#### S17. Cryogenic slow-mind
- **Origin cradles:** cold hydrocarbon worlds — Titan-analog haze world (#37), Barnard's Star b (#31), cold outer moons.
- **Medium & body plan:** cryogenic; **methane/ethane** as solvent instead of water; low-energy, slow-growing bodies.
- **Senses & signal:** thermal and chemical sensing in a dim, hazed world; slow chemical or acoustic signals.
- **Sociality & cognition:** sparse; **glacially slow** cognition — a thought can take a season.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation possible, but the metabolic tempo is so slow that a "technological civilization" here operates on a timescale of its own — deep patience is not a virtue but a condition. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Depth**, **Curator** (patience beyond reckoning), lean **Silence**.

#### S18. Radiation-hardened surface extremophile
- **Origin cradles:** irradiated worlds — UV-scoured F-star world (#28), coreless silicate world (#35), Proxima b (#3), runaway-greenhouse-edge (#27).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial, heavily **shielded** — pigmented, mineral-armored, or thick-walled; built to take a beating from the sky.
- **Senses & signal:** UV-shifted or shielded vision; robust chemistry; high cellular turnover and repair.
- **Sociality & cognition:** hardy groups; unitary cognition under constant environmental assault.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** manipulation and fire available; the antagonist is the sky itself and the short clock of a bright, fast-living star — a lineage that climbs *fast* or not at all. **Technogenesis: Hard.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Silence** (a sky that hurts), **Shedder** (constant damage-and-repair, a fast turnover of the past), lean Reach under a short clock.

#### S19. High-temperature mineral life
- **Origin cradles:** the extreme worlds — 55 Cancri e (#26), carbon world (#34), super-Mercury (#33 hot side).
- **Medium & body plan:** subsurface or deep-crust; **high-temperature chemistry** (carbon-rich, possibly part-mineral); no surface existence at all.
- **Senses & signal:** thermal and chemical/seismic sensing in rock and melt; signalling by heat and vibration.
- **Sociality & cognition:** the most speculative case — cognition on a mineral or high-carbon substrate, deeply alien in tempo and form.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** almost everything is against it; a technological civilization here is at the outer bound of plausibility, included as the extreme edge the game can gesture at rather than lean on. **Technogenesis: Near-impossible.**
- **Dial seed:** **Depth**, **Silence**, deeply alien on every axis.

### G. Photo-motile minds

*The seam between plant and animal: organisms that eat light but move and choose.
A dim red sun makes this pay, and a strange, gentle intelligence can grow from it.*

#### S20. Photovore
- **Origin cradles:** dim-lit worlds — Teegarden's Star b (#7), TRAPPIST-1f (#2), eyeball ice world (#14), Kepler-186f (#9).
- **Medium & body plan:** terrestrial-or-aquatic, **part-photosynthetic and mobile** — grazes light, roots to bask, moves to chase the sun across a dim sky.
- **Senses & signal:** exquisite light-sensing (every photon counts under a red dwarf); slow visual/chemical signalling.
- **Sociality & cognition:** stands and colonies that are half-forest, half-society; slow, gentle, collective-leaning cognition.
- **Manipulation & path to technology:** weak manipulation and a slow, light-starved metabolism make the climb long and uphill; when it comes, it is patient and grown rather than forged. **Technogenesis: Severe.**
- **Dial seed:** strong **Depth** and **One Mind**, lean **Custodian** (a species for whom life quite literally *is* light).

**Coverage check.** The catalog is meant to span the whole axes-of-variation
space, and the table confirms it does:

- **Medium** — all five are populated: aquatic (S1–S5, plus vent-dwelling S16),
  terrestrial (S6–S8, S11–S13, S15, S18, S20), aerial/buoyant (S9, S10),
  subsurface/sealed (S15, S16, S19), and cryogenic (S17). The pool skews aquatic
  and terrestrial exactly as habitable worlds do.
- **Cognition model** — every fork appears: unitary (the plurality — S2–S4, S6–
  S9, S15–S18), distributed (S1, S10), collective (S11, and collective-leaning
  S20), networked (S12), composite (S14), emergent-colonial (S5), and
  on-demand-aggregate (S13). The One Mind ↔ Chorus dial is therefore pre-loaded
  from *both* ends by biology, as *How this feeds Act 1* and the open questions
  note.
- **Technogenesis** — Straightforward ×2 (S6, S7 on its surface) · Hard ×7 (S3,
  S8, S9, S11, S14, S15, S18) · Severe ×6 (S1, S4, S13, S16, S17, S20) ·
  Near-impossible ×5 (S2, S5, S10, S12, S19). The distribution mirrors the
  cradle tiers in act1-cradles.md: most roads to technology are genuinely hard,
  a handful are near-barred, and the easy climb is deliberately rare — the same
  shape as the difficulty pool.
- **Dial ends** — all ten leaning ends are seeded by at least one body: **Reach**
  (S9, lean S3/S18), **Depth** (S1, S4, S5, S7, S10, S12, S15–S17, S19, S20),
  **Voice** (S2, S8, S9, faint S6), **Silence** (S4, S10, S15–S19), **Custodian**
  (S2, S5, S14, lean S7/S20), **Instrumental** (S3, S11, lean S4/S8/S16),
  **One Mind** (S5, S7, S8, S11, S12, S20), **Chorus** (S1, S13, S14, lean
  S2/S10), **Curator** (S12, S17), and **Shedder** (S13, S18). No dial end is
  left without a biological source before Act 1's branches move the point.

## How this feeds Act 1

This catalog is not just worldbuilding — it is the raw material of the Act 1
branching history and the seed of the Act 2 dials.

- **A cradle breeds several candidates.** The mapping table below gives each of
  the 40 cradles its plausible lineages. Early Act 1 beats are, in part, the
  story of **which lineage inherits the world** — and the bottleneck beats
  (gameplay-walkthrough.md, *"the two kinds of choice"*) are where you back one
  and let another fall, or shelter a doomed lineage through the gap.
- **The losers write the winner's values.** Whether you *sheltered* rival
  lineages or *spent* them feeds **Custodian ↔ Instrumental**; whether you
  preserved the deep past or leapt clear of it feeds **Curator ↔ Shedder**. The
  species you become is partly defined by the ones you didn't.
- **The body pre-loads the dials.** Each dial seed above is the *base* the
  biology sets before a single branch is taken — the same role the cradle plays
  in act1-cradles.md. A hive starts near One-Mind/Instrumental; a song-culture near
  Voice/Custodian; a burrower near Silence/Depth. Act 1's branches then move the
  point inside the range the cradle's harshness allows.
- **The technogenesis filter is the difficulty.** A *Near-impossible* lineage on
  a Brutal cradle is exactly why some worlds are Tier V — and it is where the
  rare *intervene* beats matter most. A world can also **fail** to raise any
  technological species; see the open questions.

## Notes and open questions

- **Some worlds may raise no one.** A Brutal cradle whose only candidate
  lineages are *Near-impossible* climbers can genuinely fail to produce a
  technological civilization. That is a legitimate Act 1 outcome and a design
  choice: does a failed climb end the run, restart the biosphere from an earlier
  beat, or become one of the galaxy's *pre-singularity* worlds that another
  player's probe finds later (vision.md, *"the recursion"*)? Open.
- **Cognition model vs. the dials.** The One Mind ↔ Chorus dial is unusually
  *pre-loaded* by biology — a hive or a network starts near One Mind, a
  distributed or composite mind near Chorus — more than by any Act 1 branch.
  Worth checking that this doesn't make that dial feel un-earned; the fix may be
  that biology sets the base and Act 1 sets an unusually *wide* range on this
  one dial specifically.
- **The tool-less dead end is a feature.** Song-cultures (S2), reefs (S5),
  drifters (S10), and substrate minds (S12) can be brilliant and never build.
  Rather than bar them, they make the **symbiotic composite** (S14) and the
  *intervene* beats meaningful — the drama of a magnificent mind that needs
  hands. Keep them.
- **Speculation is flagged, not hidden.** The exotic-chemistry minds (S17–S19)
  sit at the edge of plausibility. Like the disputed cradles in act1-cradles.md, they
  earn a place as *archetypes* while the game should lean on the well-grounded
  lineages and treat the exotic ones as rare, strange encounters.
- **Data shape (for when this leaves Markdown).** Each species already carries
  the fields a generator needs: medium, body plan, energy base, sensory suite,
  signal channel, cognition model, a technogenesis rating, and a per-dial seed.
  Together with the cradle records in act1-cradles.md, this is the seed for a typed
  `Lineage` record and a cradle→lineage weighting table when Act 1 is built.
- **Next tier.** This is the *species* level. The tiers above it — the
  post-singularity mind each lineage wakes into (Act 2), and the diverged
  descendants it becomes across deep time (Act 3) — are their own maps, and this
  catalog is where they start.
