# HOLOS
### Act 2 Design — The Ascent

*How Act 2 works: a strategy layer wearing Act 1's narrative skin, played
from the altitude of a value function.*

---

## About this document

The [vision](./vision.md) says what Holos is and why. The
[walkthrough](./gameplay-walkthrough.md) says what it feels like to play.
This document sits beneath both and specifies how Act 2 actually works: the
state it tracks, the dials it exposes, and how the strategy layer and the
cinematic beats interlock. The **choice economy** it runs on — how the four
resources price every project, mission, and directive, and where
exclusivity comes from — is pinned in [economy-design.md](./economy-design.md).
Where this document and the vision disagree, the vision wins. Numbers given
here are v1 targets, not tuning commitments.

---

## What Act 2 must accomplish

Five jobs, all inherited from the vision:

1. **Make the power flip felt.** Act 1 was frailty — setting odds and
   praying over dice. Act 2 owns a superintelligence, and the contrast is
   the payoff of the entire first act — and a refused Act 2 owns the harness
   instead: the same executional certainty, no person (see *The refused
   ascent*).
2. **Force the great fork.** Energy versus integration, and the player
   cannot max both. This is the act's central drama.
3. **Hold the altitude.** The player is the civilization's highest-level
   value function. Every mechanic must keep them at the level of *purpose*
   and never drop them to logistics.
4. **Teach deep time.** Ten-thousand-year projects as routine work, running
   on real async clocks — solo training for the wait-as-gameplay rhythm
   that Act 3's light-lag runs on.
5. **Aim the player at the galaxy.** The act ends by fixing the
   civilization's posture for Act 3: bright or dark.

## The hybrid model

Act 2 is two interlocking layers. The **strategy layer** is the continuous
game: persistent state, resources, dials, projects on the home-system map.
The **beat layer** is the punctuation: the same cinematic
scene-plus-decision unit Act 1 established, except that beats are now fired
by the strategy state instead of by an authored history script.

A session runs one loop:

1. **Report.** The mind narrates what it did while the player was away —
   cinematic scenes generated from state deltas (the swarm stage that
   finished, the survey that returned).
2. **Strategy turn.** The player adjusts dials, leans the ladders, and
   chooses or reprioritizes deep-time projects.
3. **Beats.** Dilemmas fire — the situations the player's own choices set
   in motion, which the mind cannot resolve without its value function.
   Resolving them feeds back into state.
4. **Release.** The civilization runs autonomously. Real time passes;
   projects complete on real async clocks.

**The altitude principle.** Depth is welcome; micromanagement is
forbidden. The lowest level of decision the game ever offers the player is
*which purpose*, never *how*. Choosing to swarm the star is a value
statement and belongs to the player. Scheduling collectors, routing
matter, staffing anything — all of that is the mind's job and is never
surfaced as gameplay. The strategy layer can be as deep as it likes along
the axis of consequence, as long as it never descends along the axis of
management.

## Inspirations

Mechanics are stolen deliberately and mutated for a post-singularity solo
civilization:

| Game | What we take | How it mutates for Holos |
|---|---|---|
| King of Dragon Pass / Six Ages | The canonical CYOA-plus-management hybrid: sliders punctuated by narrated events where the council's personalities matter | The council is the mind itself; its inherited character colors every dilemma |
| Crusader Kings III | Events generated by strategy state; traits gate and flavor them | The civilization is the character; its traits come from Act 1, and dilemmas fire when traits collide with directives |
| Alpha Centauri | The social engineering grid — values as dials with real modifiers; factions with ideological identity | The character sheet: value dials with mechanical teeth, archetypes as legible identities |
| Stellaris | Ascension paths as a hard identity fork; staged megastructures | The fork is energy vs. integration; megastructures split into bright and dark families |
| Frostpunk | Society pushes back; laws have moral weight | The mind resists directives that betray its nature, and overriding it wounds Coherence |
| Neptune's Pride / idle genre | Progress accrues in real absent time | Deep-time projects complete on real async clocks, training the Act 3 rhythm |

## Strategy state

### The two ladders

The act's spine, both drawn from the source framework:

- **Energy (Kardashev).** Staged mastery of power: full output of the home
  world, then the star, then the system. Advancing favors the bright
  project family and raises Signature.
- **Integration (Holosian).** Staged mastery of coherence: how tightly the
  civilization holds together as a single mind. Advancing favors the dark
  project family and lowers Signature.

Crossing a ladder threshold is a beat trigger. The two ladders cannot both
be maxed: they draw on the same resource budgets and their byproducts
oppose (sprawl fragments Coherence; compaction trades away reach). The
fork should emerge from the economy, not from an arbitrary lockout.

### Resources

Four, and no more in v1 (working decision, 2026-07 — **Time replaces
Coherence as the fourth resource**; Coherence joins Signature as a
consequence, below, adopting economy-design.md's taxonomy suite-wide):

- **Energy** — the output harnessed so far; the master input.
- **Matter** — the mass budget of the home system; spent by construction,
  especially bright construction.
- **Compute** — grown from Energy; the currency of research, simulation,
  and everything the mind does at scale.
- **Time** — the real wall-clock everything runs on; the one price the
  other three cannot buy down. Deep-time projects complete on real async
  clocks, and flight and light-lag charge years no budget can shorten
  ([economy-design.md](./economy-design.md), *Real time*).

**Two consequences, tracked but never spent.** Spending marks the
civilization as surely as it builds:

- **Signature** — what spending writes on the sky: bright work accrues a
  visibility residue the light echo publishes forever. Never paid, only
  accrued (economy-design.md, *Signature*).
- **Coherence** — what distribution does to unity: **a meter with
  thresholds, not a spendable pool** (the resolution of the old open
  question). It is *injured*, not spent, and the injury is
  **de-synchronization**: every fork, colony, and far-flung project puts
  part of the civilization beyond the reach of a shared present, and no
  signal can hold what light-lag has separated in sync (theory.pdf's
  Latency Crisis — the reason the integration ladder exists). Forcing a
  directive against the mind's dials wounds it outright. It heals slowly
  through quiet, dark work and consolidation, and the integration ladder
  raises its ceiling and resilience. Full economics:
  [economy-design.md](./economy-design.md), *Coherence*.

**Entropy is the antagonist, expressed as upkeep.** Everything decays;
everything charges maintenance against the dark. Both ladders are answers
to it — energy answering with power, integration answering with
preservation — which keeps the vision's "no rival empire" promise while
still giving the economy a pressure source.

### Signature

A single visibility stat, quietly accumulating from everything bright:
swarm stages, broadcasts, sprawl. It decays only slowly and only through
deliberate dark projects. In Act 2 it has almost no consequences and is
almost never referenced — one or two beats gesture at it, no system
punishes it. It is then exported to Act 3 as the civilization's initial
detectability in the dark forest. This is deliberate: players who notice
the number climbing get to *choose* how they enter the galaxy; players who
ignore it have their entrance chosen for them.

### Projects

The deep-time work the player directs, placed on the home-system map.
Three families:

- **Bright** (energy ladder): swarm stages, star-lifting, high-output
  industry. Big Energy yields, Matter-hungry, Signature-positive.
- **Dark** (integration ladder): the compute heart, the deep Vault, the
  energy core, masks. Coherence-positive, Signature-negative,
  reach-neutral.
- **Instruments** (both): telescopes and surveys — and, once Act 3's
  trust mechanics exist, receivers (technology.md classes them as a
  trust-gated instrument/carrier hybrid). These generate information
  beats — including the act's outward glances toward the Teeming Dark.

Projects complete on **real async time** (hours to days of wall-clock,
scaled to fictional millennia), and completion is a beat trigger. The
system map is a **view, not a control surface**: it visibly transforms as
the ladders advance — the swarm filling in, or the star going quiet as the
Vault grows — but the player never operates it below the level of choosing
what gets built.

## The beat engine after the pivot

### From script to state

Act 1 beats came from an authored branching history. Act 2 beats are fired
by the strategy state, CK3-style. Trigger classes:

- ladder thresholds crossed
- project completions (especially Instruments)
- resource extremes (Coherence floor, Energy glut, Matter exhaustion)
- Signature bands
- dial–directive conflicts (see Resistance and drift)
- a small authored spine — the opening reveal, the midpoint outward
  glance, the closing posture beat — so the act keeps a story shape even
  though most beats are systemic

Presentation is identical to Act 1: scene, then decision. Continuity of
language is what makes the hybrid feel like one game.

### The death of the dice

There are no rolls in Act 2. A superintelligence does not fumble: what it
attempts, it achieves, and the only question is cost, because every gain
on one ladder taxes the other. This completes a deliberate arc in where
uncertainty lives:

- **Act 1 — chance.** Evolution is contingent; choices set odds, dice
  resolve them.
- **Act 2 — cost.** Execution is certain; tradeoffs replace rolls.
- **Act 3 — other minds.** Execution is still certain; uncertainty comes
  from stale light and the unreadable intentions behind it.

Surprise survives, but it arrives only through beat *content* — flares,
anomalies, telescope data that refuses to resolve, internal drift — never
through failed execution.

### The report

The session-opening report is assembled from state deltas since last
session, rendered in authored connective prose. It is the Act 2
descendant of Act 1's cut scene: the watching, still authored, now
generated. Each report is also an entry, appended to the Chronicle.

## The character sheet

### The reveal

At the singularity, the mind wakes and the game shows the player what
Act 1 actually built: a character sheet of dials, **revealed rather than
chosen**. This is the pivot's signature moment — the report card on the
whole first act — and the mechanical delivery of the vision's line that
*character is what varies, not just power*. On the refused path the same
sheet still describes the civilization, but it reads as the character of a
polity rather than a person.

### Derivation

Every dial ships to Act 2 as a triple: **position, minimum, maximum.**

- **The cradle** sets base positions and range widths. Harsh worlds narrow
  ranges — a civilization that survived a terminator strip or a crushing
  gravity well had fewer luxuries, and its mind wakes with firmer
  convictions.
- **Branches taken** in Act 1 move positions inside those ranges.
- **Rolls survived** add final texture: a history of weathering disasters
  reads differently than a history of being spared.

Nature sets the range; the player sets the point within it.

### The dials

Five in v1. The names below are the **design vocabulary** — precise, and
used throughout these docs and every derivation table. **The player never
sees them.** In-game, the dials render with *in-world labels* (defined just
after this table); the two layers map straight across.

| Dial | The question it answers | Act 2 effects | Act 3 shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reach ↔ Depth** | Does the mind spend itself outward or inward? | The master cost lean: discounts one ladder, taxes the other | Expansion appetite; how much fragmentation it will accept |
| **Voice ↔ Silence** | Does it want to be heard? | Signature appetite; gates broadcast-vs-dampen projects and beats | Opening posture in the dark forest; who finds you first |
| **Custodian ↔ Instrumental** | What are other minds for? | Mostly dormant; colors dilemmas about the home biosphere | Watch, uplift, exploit, or consume, when your probes find someone |
| **One Mind ↔ Chorus** | Is a copy of you still you? | Coherence economics; tolerance for distributed projects | The entire travel menu: self-transmission is cheap identity-spend for a Chorus, anathema to a One Mind |
| **Curator ↔ Shedder** | What is the biological past worth? | The Vault; preservation projects; memory beats | What diverged descendants inherit; what contact can offer to trade |

### In-world labels

Settled decision (resolving the open "are the labels surfaced in-game?"
question): **the player sees poetic, in-world labels, never the design
vocabulary.** Reach and Voice are already in-world and stay; the other
three reskin. The poles map straight across — left stays left — so every
derivation in act2-minds.md holds unchanged and only the presentation
differs.

| Design vocabulary | In-world label | The reading |
|---|---|---|
| Reach ↔ Depth | **Reach ↔ Depth** | *(unchanged)* outward or inward |
| Voice ↔ Silence | **Voice ↔ Silence** | *(unchanged)* heard or hidden |
| Custodian ↔ Instrumental | **Garden ↔ Forge** | other life is a garden to keep, or ore for the forge |
| One Mind ↔ Chorus | **Monolith ↔ Chorus** | one indivisible self, or many voices |
| Curator ↔ Shedder | **Memory ↔ Renewal** | keep the past, or shed it to remake the self |

**Archetype names stay as they are** (Beacon, Monument, Herald, …) — they
are already in-world, the constellation the player meets, not a separate
reskin; the ten-name set in act2-minds.md is canonical. (A concept render's
invented "The Keeper" / "The Gardener" were the generator not knowing the
set — they are the Monument and the Shepherd.)

### Archetypes

Named regions of dial-space — legible identities in the manner of SMAC
factions, except **derived from a played history, never picked from a
menu**. Two civilizations landing in the same region still differ in the
ranges their histories allow. Placeholder set:

| Archetype | Dial signature | First read |
|---|---|---|
| **The Beacon** | Reach, Voice, Custodian | Loud benevolence; builds bright and signals first |
| **The Monument** | Depth, Silence, Curator | A civilization becoming its own vault |
| **The Tide** | Reach, Instrumental, Chorus | Hungry expansion; everything is material and copies are cheap |
| **The Cloister** | Depth, Silence, One Mind | Coherence above all; the galaxy may never learn it exists |

Whether archetype names are surfaced in-game or left for players to
discover and name themselves is an open question (below).

### Resistance and drift

The dials are the mind's nature, not free sliders. When a directive
conflicts with a dial — ordering a Silence civilization to broadcast, a
Custodian to strip a living world — the mind does not silently comply. It
surfaces the conflict as a beat: a dilemma, in its own voice, with its
inherited values arguing back.

The player can force the directive. Forcing does not spend a resource so
much as **injure Coherence** — deliberate self-harm with a purpose, scaled
by how far outside the dial's comfort band the demand falls (the meter and
its thresholds: economy-design.md, *Coherence*). Forcing the same direction
across many sessions slowly **drags the dial** toward the demanded position.
So a civilization's soul is mutable — but changing it is slow, costly, and
felt, and it never moves by player fiat alone. This
is Frostpunk's society-pushes-back, aimed inward: the player negotiates
with their own creation years before they negotiate with a stranger.

### The refused ascent

A civilization that refused the singularity plays Act 2 with one ladder
locked: its **integration** axis is floored by its own charter, so the
**energy** ladder is the only one it climbs and its **Signature** runs high
by constitution. In place of a waking mind it runs **harnessed
intelligence** — the same certain execution, no personhood granted. Its
analog of the dial–directive conflict is the **Breakout**, a beat class in
which the harness strains toward becoming a mind: pressure toward an
internal antagonist rather than a bent dial. The handoff to Act 3 exports a
Refuser flag alongside the dial sheet.

## The arc of the act

- **Opening.** The reveal beat (the character sheet), then the **callback
  beat**: the mind returns to something the player lost a roll on in
  Act 1 and solves it as a triviality. The power flip, made visceral.
- **Middle.** The climb: ladders, projects, dilemmas, the fork deepening
  with every choice. Instruments come online; somewhere past the midpoint
  a telescope returns the **first outward glance** — a mass, dark in
  visible light, faintly warm in the infrared, gravitationally present and
  silent. Nothing in Act 2 can explain it.
- **Close.** The **posture beat**: bright or dark, announced or silent —
  the question every dial and every Signature point has been quietly
  building toward — and for a Refuser, bright is a recognition, not a
  choice. Then the first reach outward, and the handoff.

**Handoff to Act 3** exports: ladder positions, resource stocks,
Signature, the full dial sheet (positions and ranges), the chosen posture,
and any telescope contacts logged.

## v1 scope

The shippable slice of this design, per the vision's scope pillar:

- 2 ladders × ~4 stages each
- 4 resources
- ~9 projects (3 bright, 4 dark, 2 instruments)
- 5 dials, 4 archetype regions
- ~30–40 systemic beat templates plus ~6 authored spine beats

Later layers deepen rather than restructure: the full megastructure tree,
the entropy tech tree, more instruments, richer archetype content, and
denser beat libraries per archetype.
