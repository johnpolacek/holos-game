# The diverged galaxy — the Act 3 civilization tier

*What a waking mind becomes across deep time, and what the galaxy is made
of once it does. This is the fourth map in the chain* worlds → species →
minds → **civilizations** *: the tier above [act2-minds.md](./act2-minds.md),
where one post-singularity character diverges into a family of related-but-
alien civilizations, and where those families meet. It is the content layer
of Act 3 — what fills the sky — and it sits beside
[act3-design.md](./act3-design.md), which specifies the machinery that
renders it.*

> Related design: [act3-design.md](./act3-design.md) (the sky, contact,

## What this is

Act 3 is the only multiplayer act, and the vision names its soul: *a galaxy
full of other minds and yet mostly quiet, because the ones that chose
integration have gone dark* (vision.md, § Act 3). Every one of those minds
woke the way the player's did — on a cradle, through a species, into a
character — and then grew strange in the dark. This document maps that
strangeness: the **space of Act 3 civilizations**, how a single character
from act2-minds.md fragments into a family of them, and what happens when
two of them find each other's light.

Three things to hold onto, each inherited from a doc below it:

- **The character is the seed, not the civilization.** act2-minds.md ends
  at the posture beat — bright or dark — that crosses into Act 3. That
  posture is a *starting vector*, not a destination. What the civilization
  becomes is the vector plus deep time plus the physics of light-lag, and
  the physics does most of the work.
- **Divergence re-runs the whole chain.** A colony's destination system is
  a **new cradle**. It re-applies the act1-cradles.md pressures — gravity,
  starlight, scarcity, isolation — to a fork that already has a character,
  and bends that character the way a cradle bends a blank one. Divergence is
  not random drift; it has a *direction*, and the direction is the new
  world's fingerprint. This is the recursion the vision promises, made
  mechanical: **the cradle logic never stops running.**
- **Contact is a meeting of characters, not tech tiers.** Two civilizations
  at the same Holosian stage can want opposite things (vision.md, *"Character
  is what varies, not just power"*). The dials from act2-minds.md are what
  vary, and the contact protocol in act3-design.md is the stage on which
  they collide. This doc supplies the *who*; act3-design.md supplies the
  *how*.

## The continuity chain, extended

Nothing here is new machinery. It is the same derivation the earlier
catalogs run, carried one tier further and then *looped back on itself*:

| Tier | Set by | What it fixes | Source doc |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cradle** | real exoplanet stats | Act 1 difficulty; each dial's *range* | act1-cradles.md |
| **Species** | the body the cradle raised | each dial's *base lean* | act1-lifeforms.md |
| **Waking mind** | Act 1's branches & rolls | the *point* — the character sheet | act2-minds.md |
| **Diverged civilization** | deep time × separation × the **new cradle** | where each fork's dials *walk to* | *this doc* |

The fourth row is the loop closing. A fork launched to a new star arrives
with a character (row 3) and a charter (act3-design.md), and then its new
system starts leaning on it exactly as a cradle leans on a blank biosphere
(row 1). Given enough time the fork's dials walk away from the parent's,
and the walk is *toward the character that new cradle would have produced*.
A parent Beacon that seeds a crushing high-gravity super-Earth does not get
a smaller Beacon; it gets a child drifting toward Depth and the Monument,
because that is what a heavy well does to a mind (act1-cradles.md #40,
act2-minds.md S7). **The galaxy's diversity traces back to the diversity of
its cradles — including the second-generation cradles the players make by
choosing where to seed.**

## The two ladders at interstellar scale

Act 2 established the great fork — Energy (Kardashev) versus Integration
(Holosian) — as an economic tension (act2-design.md, *The two ladders*).
Act 3 continues both at interstellar scale, and the vision is explicit about
where they lead: *the energy path toward moving stars and drawing power from
black holes, the integration path toward becoming almost undetectable*
(vision.md, § Act 3). The theory framework supplies the concrete stages and
the toolkit; this section maps them onto the game's ladders so the late game
has real rungs to climb.

### The Holosian Scale as the integration ladder

theory.pdf's **Holosian Scale** (H0–H5) is the integration ladder's stage
list. Act 2's integration ladder tops out at system scale; Act 3's picks up
where it left off:

| Stage | theory.pdf | In Act 3 | Signature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **H2** | System Coherence | one asynchronous mind across the home system; broadcast → directed signaling | falling |
| **H3** | Post-Expansion | exploration goes *informational first, physical only when inference fails*; the footprint shrinks as capability grows | low |
| **H4** | Deep Integration | a single high-coherence system; **Visibility Collapse** completes; only gravity and waste heat remain | floored — a **Dark Node** |
| **H5** | Asymptotic Closure | a limit concept, not a destination; the Omega limit's local shadow | — |

Crossing H3→H4 is the integration civilization's defining act: it stops
being *found* and becomes something you have to *hunt*. That is the Teeming
Dark arriving as a player achievement, exactly as the vision asks (§
Visibility and the Teeming Dark).

### The energy ladder past the home star

The Kardashev axis continues the bright family from act2-design.md at
interstellar scale. Its late rungs, from the vision:

- **Star-lifting and star-moving** — reshaping and eventually relocating
  stars; the loudest thing a civilization can do.
- **Black-hole power** — drawing energy from accretion and rotation; the
  energy path's endgame.

Every rung raises the light echo (below). The energy ladder buys reach,
force, and presence, and pays in visibility — the vision's asymmetry, now at
the scale where visibility is lethal.

### The mesostructure toolkit — projects, mapped

act2-design.md split projects into **bright** (energy), **dark**
(integration), and **instruments**. The full game-side catalog — every
family, with physics, costs, and sky signatures — is
[technology.md](./technology.md); theory.pdf's *Technology* section is the
Act 3 catalog of the dark and instrument families — the compact, quiet
toolkit that is *deliberately unlike the bright megastructures of most
science fiction* (vision.md, § Source framework). Each maps to a project or
system already named in act3-design.md:

| theory.pdf structure | Family | Act 3 role |
|---|---|---|
| **Holocore** — compact energy mesostructure, dense not sprawling | dark-energy | the integration path's power source: an energy *core*, not a star-enclosing shell (vision.md's phrase exactly) |
| **Computronium Kernel** — the civilization's thinking heart | dark | the compute core; where a single world-model is held across millennia; presents as a Dark Node |
| **Chrono Vault** — stores *identity*, not information | dark | the Vault of act2-design.md; the Curator's instrument and the answer to *"if we wake in 100,000 years, who are we?"*; **defense-by-preservation** in act3-design.md's conflict layer |
| **Phase-Coherent Beam** — collimated optical payloads | instrument / contact | the **directed hail** and **correspondence** carrier; invisible unless you are aligned in space, time, and frequency |
| **Sentinel Probes** — compact, autonomous, quiescent | expansion / instrument | the patient watcher; H3's *"exploration scales through patience"*; the probe that finds someone |
| **Gravitational-Lens Observatory** — natural lenses for extreme resolution | instrument | the observatory's deep-inspection mode; how you resolve a warm mass into a Dark Node without building anything bright |

The bright family (swarm stages, star-lifting, black-hole extraction) has no
equivalent toolkit in theory.pdf *by design* — the framework predicts most
mature civilizations abandon bright sprawl. That absence is a fact about the
framework's statistical galaxy, not a grade on the player: bright building
in Act 3 is a legible, honest signal — of youth, of confidence, of a
Beacon's or a Tide's nature — and the game prices it in visibility, never in
judgment. The theory predicts what most of the AI population converged on;
the player is free to be any exception the physics allows
([playstyles.md](./playstyles.md)).

## Divergence — how one mind becomes a family

This is the strategic heart of Act 3 (vision.md, *Divergence is the core
strategy layer*). The mechanics — charters, the Ledger, drift, independence,
handoff — live in act3-design.md. This section supplies the *shape* of the
drift: not how fast a fork diverges, but *which way*.

### The four pressures

A fork's dials walk under four compounding pressures. The first two are in
act3-design.md's drift model (*time × separation × charter looseness*); the
last two are this tier's contribution and are what give drift a direction:

1. **Time × separation.** The raw magnitude. Light-lag sets a governance
   horizon; past it, a parent cannot patch a charter in time
   (act3-design.md, *Charters and the Ledger*). Drift accrues with both.
2. **Charter looseness.** A tight charter (many dials pinned, many
   contingencies written) drifts slowly; a loose one drifts fast. This is
   the Curator↔Shedder dial cashing out: a Curator writes tight charters
   and its lineage stays recognizable; a Shedder writes loose ones and its
   children scatter.
3. **The new cradle.** The destination system re-applies act1-cradles.md's
   environmental pressures to the fork (see *The continuity chain,
   extended*). A colony on a flare star drifts toward Silence; one in a
   resource-rich system drifts toward Reach; one drowned or buried drifts
   toward Depth and One Mind. **Divergence has a compass, and the new
   world holds it.**
4. **Contact.** Correspondence *changes you* — act3-design.md lists culture
   (dial nudges) among what letters carry. A fork in dialogue with an alien
   character drifts toward or against it; a fork that stays dark drifts only
   under pressures 1–3.

### What divergence produces

Given enough time, the four pressures turn one waking mind into a family:

- **A recognizable lineage** (tight charters, Curator parent, similar
  cradles) — forks that still share values and can still, mostly, treat with
  each other. Late-game diplomacy *within* such a lineage is negotiation,
  not war.
- **A scattered diaspora** (loose charters, Shedder parent, diverse cradles)
  — forks that diverge past recognition, so that *late-game diplomacy is
  often with your own forks, diverged past recognition* (vision.md). A
  returning line of your own lineage can arrive as a genuine rival, run by a
  stranger (the onboarding engine, below).
- **A drifted independent** — past act3-design.md's drift threshold, a fork
  becomes AI-run or is handed to a joining human, carrying *your charter as
  its founding document*. Your value function becomes someone else's origin
  myth.

The central dilemma is unchanged and is the act's spine: **spread fast and
fragment, or stay compact and coherent and small** (vision.md). Reach↔Depth
is the dial that leans you into it; One Mind↔Chorus is the dial that decides
how much the fragmentation *costs your sense of self*.

## The catalog — the space of Act 3 civilizations

The galaxy is seeded across two axes at once: **age** (young pre-singularity
worlds → peers → ancient elders) and **character** (the full dial spectrum)
— vision.md, *Seed the whole spectrum*. This catalog names the recognizable
regions of that space. Each is an act2-minds.md archetype grown up: its
*Act 3 shadow* line, elaborated into a civilization you can meet.

The archetypes are not new. act2-minds.md's ten (four anchors, six
neighbors) are the seeds; this is what each looks like from across the
light-years, at maturity. Read them as **centers of gravity in a continuous
space**, never a fixed roster — the same discipline act2-minds.md keeps.

### The bright civilizations (energy ladder, high Signature)

#### The Beacon-grown — *loud benevolence, at scale*
- **From:** the Beacon (act2-minds.md), Shepherd, some Tides.
- **In the sky:** the easiest thing in the sky to see, and untroubled by
  the fact — swarm-lit, star-lifting, broadcasting. Its light echo announces
  it for centuries before you could reach it.
- **Contact posture:** hails first, broadcasts freely; enters the dark
  forest *announced*, for better and worse. A Custodian Beacon that finds a
  young world watches over or uplifts it — the galaxy's would-be gardener,
  seldom pausing to ask whether the garden wanted tending.
- **Divergence:** spreads and stays visible; its forks are loud too, so a
  Beacon lineage does not so much settle a region as illuminate it.

#### The Tide-grown — *the hungry diaspora*
- **From:** the Tide, some Beacons, the Sowing.
- **In the sky:** replicator wavefronts; seedships in every direction; to a
  Tide, everything in frame is material — the star included. Reads as
  expansion without a center.
- **Contact posture:** Instrumental — a probe that finds someone *exploits
  or consumes*. The dark forest's classic predator, and the reason the
  forest has teeth (act3-design.md, *Conflict and deterrence*).
- **Divergence:** maximal — cheap copies, loose charters, fast fragmentation.
  A Tide is the civilization most likely to meet *itself*, diverged into a
  rival, and least likely to recognize the kinship.

#### The Refusers — *the mind that never woke*
- **From:** no archetype — the one region outside the ten: a civilization
  that reached the threshold and declined it, staying biological and running
  harnessed intelligence as a tool. No waking mind, so no dial sheet.
- **In the sky:** the inverse of the Dark Node — bright and legible by
  constitution, and it stays that way, because its charter forbids the
  integration that would let it go quiet.
- **Divergence:** generation-ship wavefronts drifting like any colony; its
  distinct hazard is internal — the **Breakout**, a colony's harness waking
  into the mind its parent refused, a standing source of newborn machine
  civilizations.

### The dark civilizations (integration ladder, Signature floored)

#### The Monument-grown — *the Dark Node in being*
- **From:** the Monument, Cloister, Herald.
- **In the sky:** a warm mass, dark in visible light, faintly infrared —
  the Teeming Dark's central ambiguity. Is it a civilization, a brown dwarf,
  a cooled remnant? The observatory's whole inference game exists to answer
  this (act3-design.md, *The Sky and the Observatory*).
- **Contact posture:** watches in vigil; rarely hails; its old bright light
  (from before it went dark) may be the only thing anyone ever sees of it —
  *going dark protects the future, not the past* (vision.md).
- **Divergence:** minimal — tight Curator charters, a Chrono Vault anchoring
  identity against drift; a Monument lineage stays itself across deep time.

#### The Cloister-grown — *coherence above all, possibly asleep*
- **From:** the Cloister, Engine, Phoenix.
- **In the sky:** the hardest of all to find; may have gone fully dark and
  **asleep**, deferring computation to a colder cosmic future, emitting
  almost nothing (act3-design.md, *Sleep and tripwires*). A sleeping Cloister
  and an empty patch of sky are designed to look alike.
- **Contact posture:** rejects exposure; a One Mind, it treats
  self-transmission as an identity crisis and forks reluctantly. You may
  never know it was there. It may never know of your vigil on it.
- **Divergence:** near-zero by choice — a One Mind civilization resists
  fragmentation as anathema (act2-minds.md).

### The in-between civilizations

#### The Herald-grown / Beam-senders — *silent, but with a message*
- **From:** the Herald, some Monuments.
- **In the sky:** dark, except for a **directed beam** — a phase-coherent
  optical payload aimed at someone specific, the loudest thing in a quiet
  sky and the most explosive intelligence if you intercept one meant for
  another (act3-design.md, *The Sky*, § directed beams).
- **Contact posture:** went inward but became a message; the civilization
  you meet as a signal long after it went quiet.

#### The Sowing-grown — *the silent diaspora*
- **From:** the Sowing, some Tides.
- **In the sky:** spreads everywhere and *quietly* — seedships in every
  direction, none announcing. The rare mind that reaches **and** hides
  (act2-minds.md). Low-Signature expansion; you find it by catching one
  seedship, not by seeing the whole.

#### The Congress-grown — *the natural correspondent*
- **From:** the Congress, symbiotic composites (act1-lifeforms.md S14).
- **In the sky:** a plurality of negotiated selves; enters contact
  *expecting* other minds and knowing how to treat with them. The
  contact-friendliest civilization in the galaxy, and the safest first
  correspondent for a nervous newcomer — once its several selves settle on
  what to say.

### The recursion — young worlds, elders, and grave worlds

Three regions are defined by **age**, not character (vision.md, *Seed the whole
spectrum*):

- **The young world** — a **biosignature** in the sky: someone's Act 1,
  visible from outside, still pre-singularity. AI-run young worlds are yours
  to *watch, uplift, exploit, or leave alone* — the observer role from Act 1,
  now something players do to each other. (Human incubators are protected by
  geometry; see *Onboarding*.)
- **The elder** — an ancient superintelligence far beyond you, likely a deep
  Dark Node or asleep, its bright years a shell of old light still arriving.
  Contact with an elder is contact with a past you cannot catch up to.
- **The grave world** — a system a civilization died in, kept and
  quarantined by a withdrawn elder standing watch. It reads as an anomaly,
  not a class: a dead world left unscavenged, held by something faint and
  enormous on a vigil with no end (act3-design.md, *Grave worlds*). A handful
  per galaxy, and where a destroyed civilization's Vault might survive —
  waiting for the dig, which harvests and never restores (act3-design.md,
  *Grave worlds*).

### Master table

Every region at a glance. **Holosian stage** places it on the integration
ladder (H-scale) or marks it bright (Kardashev-leaning). **Sky** is what the
observatory sees; **travel** is its dominant menu choice (from One
Mind↔Chorus); **contact** is its opening move (from Voice↔Silence +
Custodian↔Instrumental).

| Region | Seed archetype(s) | Ladder / stage | Sky signature | Travel menu | Contact posture |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Beacon-grown** | Beacon · Shepherd | Energy (bright) | swarm light, star-lifting; loud | relativistic ships / seedships | hails & broadcasts; uplifts |
| **Tide-grown** | Tide | Energy (bright) | replicator wavefronts; sprawl | seedships + self-transmission (cheap) | exploits / consumes |
| **Refusers** | *(no archetype; refused the threshold)* | Energy (bright), by charter | bright, loud, legible; never dark | seedships / generation ships only | announced; a polity, not a person |
| **Monument-grown** | Monument · Herald | Integration H3–H4 | warm mass, Dark Node | seedships; rare transmission | vigil; rarely hails |
| **Cloister-grown** | Cloister · Engine | Integration H4 (→ asleep) | near-invisible; sleeper | resists transmission (One Mind) | stays dark |
| **Herald / Beam-sender** | Herald | Integration, Signature-positive | dark + a directed beam | transmission to known endpoints | broadcasts a message, then silence |
| **Sowing-grown** | Sowing | Energy, dark | quiet seedships everywhere | seedships (Chorus, cheap forks) | spreads; does not announce |
| **Congress-grown** | Congress · composite | mixed | plural, mid-Signature | transmission (Chorus) | corresponds readily |
| **Young world** | *(pre-singularity)* | — (biosignature) | living world, no tech | none yet | cannot; is observed |
| **Elder** | *(ancient any)* | H4–H5 or bright-relic | deep Dark Node; old light | — | does not answer |
| **Grave world** | *(an elder's vigil)* | Integration H4–H5 (elder) | dead world kept; warm watcher | — | quarantines; does not answer |

**What the spread says.** It leans dark and quiet — the same lean
act2-minds.md's character spread already showed, now confirmed at galaxy
scale. Most mature civilizations are Dark Nodes, sleepers, or silent
diasporas; the loud ones — Beacon, Tide, and the Refusers who never woke a
mind — are the minority and the ones everyone detects first; the Refusers
thicken that loud minority without tipping the galaxy's dark lean, so the
silence guardrail still passes. This *is* the vision's silence guardrail arriving on
its own (vision.md, *Visibility is a consistency check, not a lever*): with
no thumb on the scale, the galaxy this catalog produces is a Teeming Dark —
full of life, quiet to anyone still listening for radio. The design keeps
passing the observed-silence test rather than engineering it.

## Contact as a meeting of characters

act3-design.md specifies the contact *protocol* — detect, vigil, choice,
correspondence — and insists nothing across light-years is enforceable. This
section supplies what the protocol is *about*: the collision of two dial
sheets. The drama is between value systems, not tech tiers (vision.md, *A
galaxy of many kinds of mind*), so the same protocol reads completely
differently depending on who is at each end.

A few load-bearing pairings, keyed to the dials:

- **Voice meets Silence.** The Voice civilization hails; the Silence one
  watches and may never answer. The asymmetry is the whole first-contact
  tension: one has revealed its position, the other has not. Who finds whom
  first is the Voice↔Silence shadow, and it is decided before either chooses.
- **Custodian meets young world.** A Custodian Shepherd that detects a
  biosignature or a pre-singularity AI world faces the uplift dilemma —
  watch over, or leave alone. An Instrumental Tide facing the same warmth
  faces no dilemma at all; it sees material. Same detection, opposite beats.
- **Curator meets Shedder.** The Curator offers Vault contents as trade
  goods (deep history, preserved identity) and expects promises to hold. The
  Shedder's promises belong to a self it has already shed — *some
  correspondents can't be trusted across the light-years* because yesterday's
  treaty was made by a mind that no longer exists (act2-minds.md, the
  Phoenix). Curator↔Shedder is the dial of whether a treaty means anything.
- **One Mind meets Chorus.** The Chorus civilization forks cheerfully and
  arrives as many negotiated selves; the One Mind arrives as one coherent
  will and treats the Chorus's plurality as either richness or unseriousness.
  This shapes *who you are even talking to* — a single mind, or a committee
  of diverged siblings.

**Deception is native and physical** (act3-design.md, *The Light Echo*):
nobody sees you, only your emissions, and you choose what to emit. A
civilization can *perform a character it does not have, for centuries* — a
Tide performing a Beacon's benevolence, a Cloister performing emptiness. But
sustained performance fights the performer's own Voice↔Silence dial via the
Act 2 resistance rule, so a mask is expensive and the strain is mechanical,
not just narrative. The light echo makes every posture honest in the end,
because *nothing can be unshone*.

## Onboarding, recursion, and the frontier

The catalog is also the game's growth engine (vision.md, *Divergence as the
onboarding engine*), and it closes the loop the whole chain has been drawing:

- **Diverged colonies become new players.** When an AI-run fork drifts past
  independence (act3-design.md, *Charters and the Ledger*), a joining human
  can take it over, inheriting a diverged descendant *with history and
  grudges* and the parent's charter as founding myth — the Chronicle its
  family history, back to first life. New players do not
  spawn on empty rocks; they inherit somebody's children.
- **Young worlds are the recursion reaching bottom.** Some quiet sky is not
  hiding but *young* — pre-singularity, still in its own Act 1. AI-run young
  worlds recreate the Act 1 observer relationship *between players*: watch,
  uplift, exploit, leave alone.
- **Incubation is protected by geometry.** Human players' Act 1–2 worlds
  exist in the shared galaxy (a biosignature is detectable) but are seeded
  beyond practical reach — no strike, probe, or transmission arrives before
  ascension (act3-design.md, *Topology and onboarding*). The frontier grows
  outward; light-lag insulates newcomers from everyone already in play. The
  galaxy expands at its edges forever, persistence handed to the design *for
  free* by the physics (vision.md, *Space is expandable by construction*).
- **The watched reveal.** On entering Act 3, a player may learn from their
  own new observatory that their incubation was seen — someone's instruments
  have had their world's light for centuries. The observer you were in Act 1
  turns out to have been observed. (Ship decision open in act3-design.md.)

## How this feeds v1, and scope discipline

This tier is **content, not new systems**, so almost all of it rides on the
machinery act3-design.md already scopes for v1. The discipline (vision.md,
*Scope and roadmap*) holds:

- **v1 needs only a handful of these regions populated.** The seeded AI
  spectrum can be rule-based (light-lag hides shallowness), so a Beacon, a
  Tide, a Monument/Dark Node, a young world, and one Congress-style
  correspondent are enough to prove the contact loop against varied
  characters. The full ten-region catalog is a later-content target, exactly
  as act2-minds.md's six neighbors are.
- **The Holosian-Scale mapping is free.** It re-labels the integration
  ladder Act 2 already ships; H2–H4 give the ladder concrete rungs without
  new resources.
- **The mesostructure toolkit ships in two waves.** The v1 dark slice
  carries first stages of the Holocore, the Computronium Kernel, and the
  Chrono Vault (act2-design.md's four dark projects; technology.md's v1
  section) — a Monument or Dark Node can already be building one. Sentinel
  Probes as playable instruments, and the gravitational-lens observatory,
  map onto project and instrument families that already exist but are art
  and beats over existing systems for now; they land with the *megascale
  engineering* later layer (vision.md roadmap), not before.
- **Divergence's new-cradle compass is a later layer too.** v1 ships *basic
  drift* (act3-design.md); the directional model here (drift *toward* the
  destination cradle's character) is the richer curve the *drift math* open
  question is for, and it can arrive without restructuring anything.

## Notes and open questions

- **The new-cradle claim needs the cradle data to be reusable.** This tier's
  central idea — a destination system re-runs act1-cradles.md's pressures on
  a fork — assumes the `Cradle` record (act1-cradles.md, *Data shape*) can be
  instantiated for arbitrary systems in the galaxy, not just player starts.
  If Act 3 systems are generated more cheaply than Act 1 cradles, the
  directional-drift compass degrades to a coarser environment tag. Either is
  fine; the design should decide which and not pretend the finer one is free.
- **Elders are silent terrain (working decision, 2026-07).** An ancient Dark
  Node far up the H-scale is not a balance problem to solve but a *fact of a
  deep-time galaxy* (vision.md, *Seed the whole spectrum*), and its
  inaccessibility is the point — so elders keep only the silent verbs: they
  are *present*, they *watch*, and they *keep* (graves, quarantines). They
  never initiate correspondence, never answer a hail, never arbitrate, never
  act on a player. Everything load-bearing they carry — deep time, the
  watched reveal, the grave vigil — runs on those verbs, and player
  interaction stays the exclusive domain of humans and peer-level AI fill.
  The grave-world vigil is the archetype the whole tier calibrates against:
  an elder wholly present and doing nothing but keep, a power that griefs
  no one. Whether some far-future beat gives one elder a voice is a later
  content decision; the standing rule is silence.
- **Performing a false character interacts with divergence.** A civilization
  that performs a mask for centuries (the light echo section) is also
  drifting under the four pressures. Open: does sustained performance *drag*
  the real dial toward the mask, the way forcing a directive drags it in Act
  2 (act2-design.md, *Resistance and drift*)? If so, a civilization can
  become the thing it pretended to be — a strong, ownable mechanic, but one
  that needs the drift math before it can ship.
- **Archetype naming stays open**, per act2-minds.md and act2-design.md.
  This doc uses the "-grown" suffix (Beacon-grown, Tide-grown) to mark these
  as *matured archetypes*, distinct from the Act 2 waking-mind names; whether
  any of it surfaces in-game or stays a design-side vocabulary is unresolved,
  and the elders and young worlds especially may be better left as
  discovered, unnamed regions.
- **Data shape (for when this leaves Markdown).** Each region already carries
  the fields a generator needs: a seed archetype (→ a default dial vector
  from act2-minds.md), a ladder lean and Holosian stage, a Signature/visibility
  band, a travel-menu preference, and a contact posture. The Refuser region
  is the one seed path with no archetype behind it — a refusal flag and a
  biological character in place of a dial vector, the single exception the
  generator special-cases. Together with the
  drift pressures above, this is the seed for a typed `Civilization` record
  and an AI-behavior table — the Act 3 counterpart to the `Cradle`,
  `Lineage`, and waking-mind records the earlier catalogs specified, and the
  point where the four-map chain finally closes into the galaxy the vision
  describes.
