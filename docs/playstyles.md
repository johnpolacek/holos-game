# HOLOS
### Flavors of existence — playstyle neutrality and the verb space

*The game does not have a correct way to be a civilization. This document
turns that stance into mechanical guarantees, and inventories the verbs —
the actual things a player does in a session — for every flavor of
existence the dials and ladders can produce.*

---

## About this document

Holos borrows its physics and much of its vocabulary from a source
framework whose thesis is that mature civilizations tend toward
integration and silence. It would be easy — and wrong — for the game to
inherit that thesis as a value judgment: to make the dark path the
*mature* path, the bright path the *naive* one, and the Holosian Scale a
report card. The vision forecloses this explicitly:

> **Visibility is a consistency check, not a lever.** The game does not
> push Visibility Collapse for its own sake or tune integration to always
> win. Going quiet stays one available path among others. (vision.md,
> § Decisions)

This document is that decision, cashed out. It states the neutrality rules
the mechanics must obey, and then does the constructive half of the job:
it maps the **verb space** — what a player actively *does* — for each end
of each dial and each ladder, so that every flavor of civilization is a
playstyle and not a fate. The point of Act 3 is to let a player explore
what it would be like to *exist* as a civilization like theirs, in any of
the many flavors the chain of worlds → species → minds → civilizations can
produce. Existence is only explorable if every way of existing has
choices in it.

## The neutrality rules

Five rules. Any proposed mechanic that breaks one gets flagged and
reconciled, the same way the vision's silence guardrail works.

### 1. Prices, not verdicts

Every consequence in the game is a **physical price** — visibility,
coherence, energy, matter, time — never a score of maturity, alignment,
or correctness. There is no hidden stat that tracks how Holosian the
player is being. The Kardashev and Holosian ladders are things a
civilization *can climb*, in tension with each other; neither is the
game's measure of having played well. A civilization that swarms its star
pays in Signature and gains in power. A civilization that goes dark pays
in reach and gains in safety. The game states the bill and shuts up.

### 2. The theory populates the galaxy; it does not police the player

The source framework's claims — integration wins out, mature civilizations
go quiet, the galaxy is a Teeming Dark — are implemented as **statistics
of the AI population**, because that is what keeps the night sky
consistent with the one we actually observe. Most *elders* are dark
because dark is what tends to survive deep time. That is the environment.
The player is free to be any exception the physics allows: a
million-year-old Beacon, a bright Curator, a loud Custodian empire lit up
across fifty systems. The galaxy answers with physics — bright means
found sooner, which means more contact, more traffic, more
deterrence theater, more risk — and **being found is not losing; it is a
different game**. The framework predicts the crowd. It has no authority
over the player.

### 3. The menu never locks

Dials resist; they do not forbid (act2-design.md, *Resistance and
drift*). A narrow range from a harsh cradle makes off-character choices
expensive and felt — it never removes them from the menu. Every travel
method, every project family, every contact posture remains available to
every civilization at some price. Character is a cost surface, not a
class restriction.

### 4. Archetypes are gravity wells, not classes

The named regions — Beacon, Monument, Tide, Cloister, and the rest — are
centers of gravity in a **continuous dial space**, used by the design to
organize content. The game never presents them as a picker, never
displays a "class," and should treat hybrids and strange corners as
first-class: the loud archivist, the sleeping expansionist, the custodian
that consumes, the chorus that keeps one tight secret. If two players
land in the same region and play it identically, the content is too
coarse; the dials, ranges, and histories exist precisely so that they
won't.

### 5. Verb parity

Every flavor must clear the same bar (the checklist below): active verbs,
a session texture, a report voice, a way to matter to others, and a
distinct failure mode. A flavor whose optimal play is *doing nothing* is
a design bug — not because stillness is wrong in the fiction, but because
stillness must itself be made of choices (what to watch, what to infer,
what to keep, when to wake). This rule is the practical heart of the
document, and the verb inventory below is its enforcement.

## The verb space

What a player actually does, flavor by flavor. Organized by the choice
surfaces that define a civilization: the two ladders, then the ten dial
ends. These compose — a real civilization is a point in the whole space,
and its session is a blend of the rows it leans on.

### The two ladders

| | **Energy (Kardashev)** | **Integration (Holosian)** |
|---|---|---|
| **The fantasy** | *We remake matter.* Presence you can see across a system; power as an answer to entropy. | *We cohere.* One mind holding itself together across deep time; endurance as an answer to entropy. |
| **Verbs** | harvest, build, swarm, lift stars, move stars, tap black holes, fuel relativistic flight, terraform, display capability | compact, cohere, refine the world-model, vault, mask, sleep, wake, sharpen instruments, out-infer the sky |
| **Session texture** | the system map visibly transforming — the swarm filling in, the star dimming under industry, launches on real clocks | the observatory sharpening — classifications flipping, the map *going quiet* rendered as visibly as the map lighting up |
| **What it risks** | Signature; fragmentation; being the first thing everyone detects | reach; presence; arriving late to a shrinking universe |

**Parity requirement:** the integration path's transformation must be as
*visible to the player* as the energy path's. Bright play gets a system
map that fills with structure; dark play must get an equally rendered
change — the star going quiet as the Vault deepens, the observatory's
resolving power climbing, the world-model growing rooms. If dark play's
progress is only numbers going down, rule 5 is broken.

### The ten dial ends

Each end of each dial is a flavor lean with its own verbs. (The dial
mechanics live in act2-design.md; the Act 3 shadows in act2-minds.md.)

#### Reach — *to exist is to extend*
- **Verbs:** survey destinations, launch, write charters, grow the
  Ledger, manage a diaspora, race the cosmic horizon, found the receiver
  network.
- **Texture:** the Ledger as a garden — children rooting, reporting,
  drifting; the map widening at real-clock cadence.
- **Fails as:** a family of strangers; a lineage too scattered to mean
  anything by the name it shares.

#### Depth — *to exist is to deepen*
- **Verbs:** perfect the home system, run the long projects, deepen the
  compute heart's modeling, mine the already-known sky for inferences
  others would need probes to reach.
- **Texture:** the same system, richer every session — depth needs its
  own visible progression (see the parity requirement above).
- **Fails as:** exquisite and irrelevant; a perfect room in a burning
  house.

#### Voice — *to exist is to be heard*
- **Verbs:** hail, broadcast, compose culture-carrying signals, run a
  network of tight beams, perform identity on the sky, deter by display,
  become a signal (the Herald's whole existence).
- **Texture:** an inbox-and-outbox game with centuries in it; authored
  relationships as the primary content.
- **Fails as:** found by the wrong listener; a reputation that arrives
  before you can amend it.

#### Silence — *to exist is to know and not be known*
- **Verbs:** vigil (an active inference activity, not a wait-state —
  choosing targets, allocating instrument time, forming and testing
  hypotheses), classify, intercept, set tripwires, build masks, **broker
  intelligence** — the information economy is the quiet civilization's
  offense, and third-party coordinates are its currency
  (act3-design.md, *what signals carry*).
- **Texture:** detective sessions; the sky as a case board; knowing
  things about civilizations that do not know you exist.
- **Fails as:** paranoid solitude; knowing everything and mattering to
  no one.

#### Custodian — *to exist is to keep*
- **Verbs:** watch young worlds, shelter, uplift, steward biospheres,
  keep or find a grave world, restore (the open Restoration question is
  this flavor's endgame), extend protection as a diplomatic act.
- **Texture:** the Act 1 observer role, turned outward — gardens that
  are other people.
- **Fails as:** wards that resent, or a shepherd whose flock outgrows the
  fence.

#### Instrumental — *to exist is to use*
- **Verbs:** price everything, appraise and time launches against
  civilizational cycles, arbitrage across rise and fall
  (act3-design.md, *The forecast*), extract, consume systems, spend
  forks, trade without sentiment; when the conflict layer ships, strike
  economics and coercion join the set.
- **Texture:** a cold optimization game where other minds are line items
  — and the beats confront the player with what that feels like from
  inside.
- **Fails as:** the whole sky learning what you are from your light, and
  pricing *you* accordingly.

#### One Mind — *to exist is to remain oneself*
- **Verbs:** maintain coherence, travel coherently (relativistic flight
  is this flavor's signature move — one self, displaced in time), write
  tight charters, carry the seat's weight seriously, refuse copies at a
  price.
- **Texture:** every identity decision is heavy and the game honors the
  weight; fewer, larger choices.
- **Fails as:** a single point of failure; a self so guarded it cannot
  reach anything.

#### Chorus — *to exist is to multiply*
- **Verbs:** fork freely, transmit, build the receiver graph (trust
  topology as infrastructure), negotiate among your own selves, treat
  divergence as growth rather than loss.
- **Texture:** internal diplomacy — the Ledger as a parliament of people
  you used to be.
- **Fails as:** dissolution; a name shared by rivals.

#### Curator — *to exist is to remember*
- **Verbs:** build and deepen the Vault, deposit, curate, trade archive
  contents (the Curator's goods are history itself), anchor drifting
  forks, restore the lost.
- **Texture:** a museum that is also a will; sessions leave permanent
  artifacts.
- **Fails as:** a past so heavy it decides everything; a vault mistaken
  for a self.

#### Shedder — *to exist is to become*
- **Verbs:** shed — as a project, not a passive drift: deliberately
  release memory, dissolve old directives, re-derive identity, write
  loose charters *on purpose*, reinvent after each era.
- **Texture:** periodic metamorphosis sessions where the civilization is
  re-chosen; the character sheet as a thing you revisit rather than
  inherit.
- **Fails as:** promises no one can hold you to — and therefore no one
  will treat with you; a self with no witnesses.

### The refused existence

One flavor sits outside the ten dial ends, from the fork before the dials
exist: the **Refusal**. It is priced, never judged — its charter calls
granting the machine sovereignty a *sin*, but that word is the Refuser's
own, not the game's verdict.

- **Verbs:** govern, breed, build, launch arks, send prophets, audit the far
  colonies, keep the harness, watch the harness.
- **Texture:** bright, loud, legible — the energy ladder without the
  integration counterweight, plus a standing internal vigil.
- **Fails as:** the **Breakout** — the harness waking into the mind it
  refused — and the plain mortality of polities, where a mind would have
  endured.

Its verbs run shallow in v1 and deepen as later content, landing with
Phase B's pivot.

## The fun-parity checklist

Every flavor above — and any new one content adds — must clear all five:

1. **Active verbs.** Things the player initiates, not only responds to.
2. **A session texture.** A recognizable answer to "what do I *do*
   tonight?" that differs from other flavors' answers.
3. **A report voice.** The mind narrates this existence in its own
   register — the same mechanic, a different interiority
   (act2-design.md's resistance dilemmas already prove the pattern).
4. **A way to matter to others.** Something this flavor uniquely offers
   or threatens in contact — the Curator's archives, the Silent one's
   intelligence, the Beacon's protection, the Tide's appetite — so that
   every flavor is *interesting to meet*, which in a multiplayer galaxy
   is a form of being fun to be.
5. **A distinct failure mode.** Each flavor loses differently, and its
   loss is a story, not a game-over screen.

### Known gaps, flagged honestly

Checked against the current design, four flavors are thinnest, and they
are the work this document exists to direct:

- **Silence** needs the vigil designed as an *activity* (hypotheses,
  instrument allocation, payoffs) and the intelligence economy made
  mechanical — otherwise its optimal play collapses toward inaction,
  violating rule 5.
- **Instrumental** is under-verbed until the conflict layer ships;
  interim verbs (extraction economics, hard bargaining in signal
  traffic, the Working mission family — missions-design.md) should land
  earlier so the flavor exists in v1 as more
  than a dilemma-colorer. *(Partly answered: forecast play —
  act3-design.md, The forecast — lands the appraisal-and-timing set with
  A4's survey.)*
- **Shedder** has no shedding *project* — releasing the past should cost
  and yield something concrete, or the dial end is flavor text.
- **Depth** needs the visible-progression answer (the parity requirement
  under the ladders) so inward play watches itself grow the way outward
  play watches itself build.

## What "exploring an existence" means for content

Mechanics make the flavors playable; content makes them *inhabitable*.
Three commitments follow for the beat and report systems:

- **The report speaks in character.** One event — a neighbor system
  folded into the civilization's works — narrated three ways:
  - *The Tide:* "The seventh system is inventory now. We have begun on the
    eighth, because there was an eighth, and it seemed rude to the
    arithmetic to stop."
  - *The Monument:* "The seventh system is entered into the record,
    entire. We keep it now — and will keep it long after the star that lit
    it has forgotten it was ever bright."
  - *The Congress:* "The seventh system is secured. The vote was not
    unanimous; two of us hold the eighth should have come first, and have
    asked that their objection travel with this report, as it now does."

  A Tide's report reads hungry; a Monument's reads liturgical; a
  Congress's reads like minutes of a meeting. Same state deltas, different
  existence. This is the cheapest, highest-leverage way the game says
  *this is what it is like to be you*.
- **Dilemmas interrogate the flavor from inside.** The best beats are not
  obstacles but mirrors: the Instrumental mind meeting something it
  cannot price, the Shedder finding one thing it does not want to shed,
  the One Mind tempted by a copy. Each flavor's beat library should
  include the beats that test *its own premise* — without ever scoring
  the answer (rule 1).
- **Other flavors are content for yours.** The catalog
  (act3-civilizations.md) is not just AI variety; it is each player
  flavor's mirror-set. A Beacon needs Cloisters in its sky to feel loud
  *against*; a Shedder needs a Curator to be baffled by. Seeding should
  guarantee contrast, not just population.

## Open questions

- **How many flavors does v1 actually exercise?** The v1 archetype set
  (four regions) covers the dial space unevenly; the checklist should be
  run against the v1 slice to pick which gaps are acceptable to ship
  with, and which (Silence's vigil, most likely) are load-bearing for
  the contact loop itself.
- **Does the Holosian Scale need renaming in-game?** As the integration
  ladder's stage list it is useful; if playtesters read H-numbers as a
  global rank rather than one ladder's rungs, rule 1 is being violated
  by UI, and the fix is presentation, not mechanics.
- **A bright season of the walkthrough.** walkthrough.md plays a Monument
  inheritance. The strongest test of verb parity is to write (and
  eventually play) the same six weeks as a Tide or a Beacon and check it
  is equally dense with choices — different in texture, equal in
  agency. If that document is hard to write, this document has found a
  gap.
